# Travel Preferences & Planning Rules

This file is the source of truth for how the Japan trip should be planned. Phase 2 per-stop research agents must read this before drafting any schedules or recommendations.

## Travelers

- **Clemens** (the 40th-birthday traveler). German passport. From Tallinn, Estonia. Opinionated traveler, depth over breadth, dislikes generic / over-touristed picks.
- **Sonja** — partner. Travels with Clemens.

## The trip is built around Clemens

Sonja's interests are real and should be honored, but Clemens's preferences are the planning anchor. When trading off, favor Clemens's axis.

## Interests

### Clemens (weight order)

1. **Japanese culture, both old and new.** Old = temples, gardens, crafts, ryokan, traditional villages, kissaten, wagashi. New = Tokyo design, contemporary art and architecture, modern city life, specialty coffee, depachika.
2. **Nature.** Mountains, foliage, gardens, onsen-as-nature-experience.
3. **Animals.** Likes them but won't detour for them.

### Sonja

- Food
- Animals
- Nature
- Japanese skincare / J-beauty

## Hard rules

### No alcohol anywhere
Strip drinking-culture stops entirely. No bars, no izakaya framed around drinks, no "Golden Gai," no "Omoide Yokocho for beer," no sake pairings, no craft beer recommendations.

**Substitute** with non-alcohol equivalents that preserve the cultural depth:
- Kissaten (retro coffee houses)
- Specialty third-wave coffee
- Jazz cafés (listening, not drinking)
- Late-night food spots (yakitori for the food, not the drinks)
- Wagashi and dessert parlors
- Depachika (department-store food halls)
- Tea-focused experiences (matcha bars, sencha tastings, tea ceremony)

### Build wander time into every day
Each city day must include at least one **2–3 hour unstructured "wander block"** — explicitly labeled as such — where Clemens and Sonja can do whatever they want, including splitting up. Not transit padding. Real free time.

- In big cities, prefer two shorter wander blocks over one large one.
- Suggest a neighborhood/area for the wander, not a checklist of things to do.
- Where it makes sense, frame as an optional split (e.g. "Sonja: J-beauty street; Clemens: record shops in Shimokitazawa").

### Depth over breadth
Pacing is *quality over quantity*. Three things done well beat seven things rushed. Cut activities sooner rather than over-pack.

### Off the tourist path
Restaurant picks must be **not famous among foreign tourists**. No Ichiran, Ippudo, Tsuta, or anything featured on TikTok / "best of Tokyo" listicles. Local-only or local-favorite spots.

### No mobility, dietary, or allergy constraints
Either traveler. Plan freely on those axes.

## The splurge moment

**Hakone ryokan, 2 nights (Oct 28–29, 2026, tentative).** This is the 40th-birthday anchor. Top-tier (Gora Kadan / Hakone Ginyu / Gora Hanaougi class). One splurge for the trip, not many.

- Animal touches that don't detour: Nara deer (day trip from Kyoto), Iwatayama monkey park if Arashiyama is on the schedule.

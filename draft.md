# Japan 40th Birthday Trip — 14 Days in Autumn

**When:** November 10–24, 2026 (sweet spot for foliage progression: Hakone at peak, Kyoto approaching peak, Tokyo starting to turn)
**Visa:** Germany = 90-day visa-free for tourism. No application needed.
**Route:** Tokyo → Kamakura → Hakone → Kyoto → Nara → Hiroshima → Miyajima → Osaka
**Transport:** Individual Shinkansen tickets (JR Pass is no longer worth it — see notes)

---

## Day-by-Day Itinerary

### Days 1–3: Tokyo

**Day 1** — Arrive Narita or Haneda. Get a Suica IC card at the airport (tap-and-go for all trains, buses, convenience stores). Train to hotel. Evening: walk Shibuya Crossing at night — the most Tokyo thing possible.

**Day 2** — East Tokyo: Asakusa (Senso-ji temple, Nakamise shopping street), walk to Tokyo Skytree for views, then Akihabara for the electric town / gaming arcade experience. Evening: Shinjuku's Golden Gai — a maze of 200+ tiny bars, each seating 6-8 people.

**Day 3** — West Tokyo: Meiji Shrine (free, serene forest in the middle of the city), Harajuku's Takeshita Street, Shibuya for shopping. Afternoon: TeamLab Borderless (book ahead, ¥3,800). Evening: Shinjuku Omoide Yokocho ("Memory Lane") for yakitori and beer under the train tracks.

> **Stay:** 4 nights in Shinjuku or Shibuya area (¥8,000–15,000/night per room)
> **Tokyo food:** Ramen (¥800–1,200), conveyor belt sushi (¥1,500–3,000), izakaya dinner (¥3,000–5,000 with drinks). Convenience store onigiri and sandwiches are genuinely excellent for ¥200–500.

---

### Day 4: Day Trip to Kamakura

Train from Tokyo (JR Yokosuka Line, ~1 hour, ¥950). A coastal temple town that was Japan's capital in the 13th century.

- Great Buddha (Kotoku-in) — a 13m bronze Buddha sitting in the open air since a tsunami washed away its temple in 1498
- Hase-dera temple — hillside gardens with ocean views
- Walk down to Yuigahama Beach for the vibes
- Komachi-dori shopping street for snacks and souvenirs

Back to Tokyo by evening. Relaxed day, good contrast to the city intensity.

> **Tickets:** Great Buddha ¥300, Hase-dera ¥400

---

### Day 5: Tokyo → Hakone

Take the Odakyu Romancecar from Shinjuku (scenic express train, ~1.5 hours, ¥2,300). Hakone is a mountain onsen town in the shadow of Mt Fuji.

**Afternoon:** Use the Hakone Free Pass (¥6,100, covers all local transport for 2 days) — cable car over the volcanic Owakudani valley, pirate ship cruise on Lake Ashi with Mt Fuji in the background (November has the clearest skies for Fuji views).

**Evening:** Check into a ryokan (traditional Japanese inn). This is a bucket-list experience: tatami rooms, futon beds, multi-course kaiseki dinner, and most importantly — soaking in an onsen (hot spring bath) overlooking autumn foliage. Budget ryokans with dinner/breakfast start around ¥15,000 per person. Splurge options run ¥25,000–40,000+ pp.

> Hakone foliage peaks early-to-mid November — you should hit it perfectly.
> **Stay:** 1 night ryokan (¥15,000–30,000 pp including dinner + breakfast)

---

### Day 6: Hakone → Kyoto

**Morning:** Last onsen soak, enjoy the ryokan breakfast (grilled fish, miso soup, rice, pickles, tamagoyaki — the works). Take the Hakone Tozan train down to Odawara station.

**Shinkansen:** Odawara → Kyoto (Hikari, ~2.5 hours, ¥11,000). Watch Japan's countryside blur past at 270km/h. On a clear day, you'll see Mt Fuji from the train — sit on the right side (seats D/E).

**Afternoon:** Arrive Kyoto. Check in, get oriented. Walk to Nishiki Market ("Kyoto's Kitchen") — a narrow 400-year-old market street with hundreds of food stalls. Try everything.

> **Stay:** 5 nights in central Kyoto, near Gion or Kawaramachi (¥8,000–12,000/night per room)
> Kyoto foliage: approaching peak around Nov 20. By your last days here it should be spectacular.

---

### Days 7–9: Kyoto

Three full days in Japan's cultural heart. Over 1,600 temples, 400 shrines, and the most concentrated autumn beauty in the country.

**Day 7 — East Kyoto:**
- Fushimi Inari Shrine (free) — the famous 10,000 orange torii gates climbing up a mountain. Go early (7am) to avoid crowds. Walk at least 30 minutes up.
- Kiyomizu-dera Temple (¥400) — wooden stage jutting out over a hillside, surrounded by maples. Possibly the most iconic autumn view in all of Japan.
- Walk through the stone-paved lanes of Higashiyama: Ninenzaka and Sannenzaka slopes, tea houses, pottery shops.
- Evening: Gion district — geisha (geiko) spotting along Hanami-koji street. They're real, not performers — be respectful, don't block their path.

**Day 8 — West Kyoto (Arashiyama):**
- Arashiyama Bamboo Grove — a towering green tunnel. Arrive at opening (8am) for near-empty photos.
- Tenryu-ji Temple (¥500) — one of Kyoto's finest zen gardens, spectacular in autumn.
- Togetsukyo Bridge — the iconic bridge with mountains of red and gold behind it.
- Monkey Park Iwatayama — hike up for panoramic views + wild Japanese macaques.
- If energy allows: Kinkaku-ji / Golden Pavilion (¥500) on the way back.

**Day 9 — Deeper Kyoto:**
- Tofuku-ji Temple (¥500) — THE premier autumn foliage temple. The Tsutenkyo Bridge creates a tunnel through a maple canopy. Late November here is mind-blowing.
- Philosopher's Path — a stone canal path lined with cherry and maple trees connecting temples.
- Nanzen-ji Temple (¥600) — massive zen temple with a Roman-style aqueduct running through it.
- Afternoon: Tea ceremony experience (~¥2,000–4,000 in Gion area).
- Evening: Check if any temples are doing autumn night illuminations (yakan tokubetsu haikan) — Kyoto lights up select temples at night during foliage season. Eikan-do and Kiyomizu-dera are legendary for this.

> Many Kyoto temples charge ¥400–600. Budget ~¥3,000 for a full day of temple-hopping.

---

### Day 10: Day Trip to Nara

Train from Kyoto (JR Nara Line, 45 minutes, ~¥720).

Nara was Japan's first permanent capital (710 AD). The main draw: over 1,000 wild deer roaming freely through the parks and temple grounds. They bow to you for food (¥200 for deer crackers).

- Todai-ji Temple (¥600) — houses the world's largest bronze Buddha inside the world's largest wooden building. The scale is staggering.
- Nara Park — endless deer, autumn trees, and open green space.
- Kasuga Taisha Shrine (¥500) — thousands of stone and bronze lanterns lining the forested approach.

Back to Kyoto by evening. This is a light, joyful day — a nice breather before the final push.

---

### Day 11: Kyoto → Hiroshima + Miyajima

**Morning:** Shinkansen Kyoto → Hiroshima (Sakura or Hikari, ~2 hours, ¥11,000).

**Late morning:** Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park and Museum (¥200). The A-Bomb Dome, the Children's Peace Monument, the museum. Allocate 2-3 hours. It's heavy but essential. One of those places that permanently shifts your perspective.

**Afternoon:** JR train to Miyajimaguchi (25 min, ¥420), then ferry to Miyajima Island (¥180, covered by JR ferry). The famous "floating" torii gate of Itsukushima Shrine appears as you approach by boat.

**Evening:** Miyajima at dusk is magical — the crowds leave with the last ferry, and you have the island nearly to yourself. Deer wander the streets, the shrine gate glows, and the mountain behind is covered in autumn colour.

> **Stay:** 1 night on Miyajima Island (¥8,000–15,000/night). Staying overnight is one of Japan's best-kept secrets — 90% of visitors are day-trippers.
> **Tickets:** Peace Museum ¥200, Itsukushima Shrine ¥300

---

### Days 12–13: Osaka

**Day 12 morning:** Ferry back from Miyajima, JR train to Hiroshima, Shinkansen Hiroshima → Shin-Osaka (~1.5 hours, ¥10,000).

**Afternoon:** Drop bags, head straight to Dotonbori — Osaka's neon-drenched food street along a canal. This is where you eat: takoyaki (octopus balls), okonomiyaki (savoury pancakes), kushikatsu (deep-fried skewers), gyoza, and anything else that catches your eye. Osaka is Japan's undisputed street food capital.

**Day 13 — Full day Osaka:**
- Morning: Osaka Castle (¥600) — a massive reconstructed fortress surrounded by a park and moat. Good autumn colours in the castle grounds.
- Afternoon: Shinsekai district — retro, chaotic, covered in neon signs. The opposite of Tokyo's polish. Eat more kushikatsu.
- Kuromon Market ("Osaka's Kitchen") — seafood, wagyu skewers, fresh uni.
- Evening: Last night out. Namba area for drinks, or find a local craft beer bar.

> **Stay:** 2 nights near Namba or Dotonbori (¥7,000–12,000/night per room)
> Osaka food is cheap and legendary. Budget ¥2,000–3,000 for a day of grazing.

---

### Day 14: Depart Osaka

Fly home from Kansai International Airport (KIX). If your flight is afternoon/evening, you have time for one more morning food crawl or a walk through the Umeda Sky Building's rooftop garden.

### Why NOT the JR Pass

The 14-day JR Pass costs ¥80,000–84,000 (€485–510). Your actual Shinkansen + JR train spend is ~¥48,000 (€290). Buying individual tickets saves you roughly €200 per person. The pass used to be a no-brainer, but after the 2023 price hike (+70%) and the October 2026 increase (+6%), it's only worth it if you're doing significantly more long-distance travel than this itinerary includes.

Buy Shinkansen tickets via the SmartEX app (English, works with foreign credit cards, reserve specific seats) or at ticket machines in stations.

## Practical Notes

**Language:** More English signage than China, but still limited outside tourist zones. Google Translate camera mode is your friend. Most train stations have English.

**Cash:** Japan is still surprisingly cash-heavy. Many small restaurants, shrines, and ryokans don't take cards. Carry ¥20,000–30,000 in cash at any time. 7-Eleven ATMs accept international cards.

**Suica/Pasmo IC Card:** Get one at the airport. Tap-and-go for all trains, buses, convenience stores, vending machines. Load it with cash at any station. This is your single most useful item.

**SIM/eSIM:** Grab a data eSIM before departure (IIJmio, Ubigi, or Airalo all work well in Japan). Data-only is fine — use LINE app for messaging (Japan's WhatsApp equivalent).

**Luggage forwarding (Takkyubin):** Japan's secret weapon. When changing cities, send your big bags ahead to your next hotel via Yamato Transport (available at convenience stores and hotels, ~¥2,000 per bag, next-day delivery). Travel with just a daypack on Shinkansen days. This is a game-changer.

**Etiquette basics:** No tipping (anywhere, ever). No eating while walking. No phone calls on trains. Bow slightly when thanking. Remove shoes when entering homes, ryokans, and some restaurants (look for the raised floor).

**Weather (mid-Nov):**
- Tokyo: 10–17°C, dry and clear
- Hakone: 5–12°C, cooler in the mountains
- Kyoto: 7–16°C, crisp and beautiful
- Osaka: 9–17°C, mild
- Bring layers. A light down jacket + scarf handles everything.

**Currency:** 1 EUR ≈ 165 JPY (verify closer to trip, the yen has been volatile)

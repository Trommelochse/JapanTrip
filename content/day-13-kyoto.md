# Day 13 — Wed, Nov 4, 2026 — Kyoto (Tofuku-ji architecture + Sanjusangen-do + Gion)

> **Theme:** Architecture-first temple morning — modernist Zen gardens at Tofuku-ji, then 1001 wooden Kannon. Slow evening in Gion.
> **Weather (typical):** 8–17°C. Likely dry, classic Kyoto crisp-clear.
> **Walking:** Medium — 6–8 km, no vertical.

## Note on Fushimi Inari (dropped)

The original plan was a 06:00 sunrise pilgrimage to Fushimi Inari to walk Senbon Torii before crowds. Under the 8:00-earliest-wake rule, the strategic value collapses — by 09:00 the shrine is "unwalkable," so a late-morning visit defeats the off-path purpose. **Fushimi Inari is dropped from this trip.** If you change your mind on the day, accept the crowds and go: it's 5 min on the JR Nara line and an unforgettable sight even packed.

## Schedule

### Morning — Tofuku-ji and the architecture chain
- **08:00** — Wake. Hotel breakfast — slow, no rush.
- **08:45** — JR Nara Line from Kyoto Station → **Tofukuji Station** · 3 min, ¥150. Walk 10 min to **Tofuku-ji**.
- **09:00** — **Tofuku-ji** (¥1,000 combined Tsutenkyo + Hojo gardens) at opening. Real talk on foliage: Tofuku-ji's maple valley peaks **late Nov, mid-Nov in a warm year**. Nov 4 will be **mostly green with first yellow/orange tips**. The Tsutenkyo bridge view will be a *green* maple sea, not red. **The architecture is the point even green:**
  - The **Hojo (abbot's quarters) gardens** designed by Mirei Shigemori in 1939 — four sides, four different gardens, the grid-checkerboard moss-and-stone one is the most famous modernist Zen garden in Japan. This is *not foliage-dependent.*
  - The **Sanmon gate** — oldest surviving Zen temple gate in Japan (1425). Massive.
  - The **Tsutenkyo bridge** — even green, it's a corridor through a maple canopy and the architecture is striking.
  Allow 90 min. At opening, the Hojo gardens are practically empty — this is the closest you'll get to the pre-crowd quiet you lost from Fushimi.
- **10:45** — Walk 5 min to **Funda-in (Sesshu-ji)** — Tofuku-ji subtemple, ¥500, almost no one goes. Has a Sesshu-designed garden (1467) and a tea house. Quiet, beautiful, takes 20–30 min.
- **11:20** — Walk 3 min to **Komyo-in** — another Tofuku-ji subtemple, ¥500, also by Mirei Shigemori (the *Hashin-tei* "wave-and-heart" stone garden, 1939). Pairs directly with the Hojo gardens as a second Shigemori example. 20 min.

### Lunch + Sanjusangen-do
- **12:00** — Lunch near Tofuku-ji or back toward Shichijo. Easiest: walk 12 min north to **Sanjusangen-do** area and eat near the Kyoto National Museum. Or take the JR one stop back to Kyoto Station and pick from the Porta basement. Quick lunch — Sanjusangen-do closes at 17:00 but you want it relaxed.
- **13:00** — **Sanjusangen-do** · ¥600 · 33-bay wooden hall (1266) housing **1,001 life-size wooden Kannon statues** in row formation, the central one a National Treasure. Indoor, breathtaking, photography forbidden. The longest wooden structure in Japan. Allow 60 min — walk the entire 120m length slowly. This was originally tucked in the rain plan; it absolutely belongs on the main schedule.
- **14:00** — Coffee/break. Walk 8 min north to **Kawai Kanjiro Memorial House** (¥900) if not done on Day 11 (closed Mon) — the folk potter's preserved home, very quiet. Or skip and head toward the wander block.

### Afternoon — central Kyoto wander
- **14:30** — Take subway/walk to **Karasuma-Oike** area. Late lunch alternative if you didn't eat earlier: **Kaneyo** (Pontocho, *unagi* specialist since 1866, lunch sets ¥3,500–6,000). Or **Yoshikawa Inn** for tempura lunch (¥6,000+, reserve via hotel). Note: **Honke Tagoto** is closed Wed.
- **14:45–16:30** — **Wander block: Nakagyo / Karasuma backstreets.** This is downtown Kyoto away from the temples. Anchor it loosely around:
  - **Ippodo Tea** (Teramachi) — Kyoto's most serious traditional tea merchant, since 1717. In-store tea bar where you can taste 3 grades of matcha or sencha (¥800–2,000). This is the tea pick, not Tsujiri.
  - **Nishiki Market** edge (skip the deep end with the lines — walk through the eastern third where the local shops are).
  - **Sou Sou** (the modern Kyoto textile brand) — fun for both of you.
  - **Inoda Coffee Honten** (Sakaimachi/Sanjo) — the kissaten. 1940 origin, dark wood, white-jacketed staff, "the Arabian Pearl" blend. Order an egg sandwich for ritual completeness. Open from 07:00 daily.
  - Sonja's J-beauty Kyoto specifics: **Kazurasei** (Gion-shijo) for camellia-oil hair products — a 160-year-old shop, not a tourist trap; the *Tsubaki-yu* hair oil is the signature. Also **Suehiro Honten** if essential oils are her thing.
- **16:30** — Coffee: **Weekenders Coffee Tominokoji** — micro-roastery hidden in a parking lot off Tominokoji street, single-origin pour-over. 7.5–18:00, closed Wed (again — verify; if closed, **Kurasu Kyoto** near the station).

### Evening
- **17:30** — **Yasaka Shrine** at dusk → walk south through **Maruyama Park** → return to Gion via Ishibei-koji (yes, again — it's that good and you didn't see it lit up Day 11).
- **18:30** — Dinner: **Gion Sasaki** (counter kaiseki, 2 Michelin) is the prestige pick but requires booking *months* ahead via hotel concierge, and the night will include sake pairings you'd be skipping. **Substitute:** **Giro Giro Hitoshina** — punk-rock kaiseki, counter seating, ¥4,500 set menu, ages-down/streetwise approach. Kawaramachi area, reservation strongly recommended (via Tabelog or hotel). Closed irregular — verify. **Or:** **Roan Kikunoi** if you want classical kaiseki without going for the flagship — Michelin 1-star, courses around ¥15,000, more accessible booking window.
  Either kaiseki: ask up front to skip the sake pairing; they will substitute *genmaicha* or non-alcoholic *amazake*. They're used to it.
- **20:30** — Walk through **Pontocho** alley once on the way back — atmosphere only, lanterns lit, very narrow, very photogenic, lots of doors you're not walking through. 10 minutes is enough.
- **21:00** — Optional last stop: **Ippodo's** in-store tea bar closes at 18:00 so it's done; instead, **% Arabica Fujii Daimaru** stays open later for a final coffee, or just head back.

## Rain plan B
Tofuku-ji + Sanjusangen-do both work fine in rain — the Hojo gardens are viewed from a covered veranda, Sanjusangen-do is entirely indoor. Skip Maruyama in evening, do the Pontocho walk under umbrella — even better in rain.

## Photography golden hour
- **Sunrise (~06:24):** Skipped on this iteration.
- **Sunset (~17:00):** Yasaka Shrine entrance lanterns coming on. Or Pontocho around 17:30 once the lanterns light but sky still has color.
- Tofuku-ji Tsutenkyo bridge from the lower-level walking platform looking up — best in soft cloud cover.
- Tofuku-ji's Hojo grid-checkerboard garden from the veranda — diffuse light, low angle, ~09:30.

## Off-path notes
- **Funda-in** (Sesshu-ji subtemple) sees maybe 5% of Tofuku-ji's visitor count and is more beautiful per square meter. Don't skip.
- **Komyo-in** (the other subtemple, now on the main schedule) — Mirei Shigemori again, same year as the Hojo. Seeing both in one morning is the architectural through-line: 1939 was the year Shigemori reinvented the Zen garden, and Tofuku-ji is the ground zero.
- **Why Sanjusangen-do moved onto the main schedule:** 1,001 wooden figures in a 120m wooden hall is not a "rain backup," it's one of the genuinely singular things to see in Japan. The original Fushimi-anchored morning didn't have room for it; the new schedule does.
- **Ippodo Tea's** ground-floor tea bar (Kaboku) is the best tea-tasting in Kyoto for an in-person experience — explicit instructions, real ceremony framing, no Instagram queue. Sit at the counter, not the salon.
- If you want a *kissaten* depth pick beyond Inoda: **Smart Coffee** on Teramachi (since 1932) does French toast that's been the same recipe for 90 years; **Tsukimochiya Naomasa** is a wagashi/coffee combination kissaten that's older than most countries.
- The "skip Pontocho" instinct from a no-alcohol angle is right for stopping in but wrong for walking through — the lane is genuinely one of the prettiest 200m stretches in Japan. Walk it; don't enter anything.

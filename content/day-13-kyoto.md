# Day 13 — Wed, Nov 4, 2026 — Kyoto (Fushimi Inari + Tofuku-ji + Gion)

> **Theme:** Pre-dawn torii gates while the rest of Kyoto sleeps. Architecture-first at Tofuku-ji even if the maples aren't peak. Slow evening in Gion.
> **Weather (typical):** 8–17°C. Likely dry, classic Kyoto crisp-clear.
> **Walking:** High — 8–10 km, plus ~250m of vertical at Fushimi (full loop) or ~100m if you stop at the Yotsutsuji viewpoint.

## Schedule

### Pre-dawn / Morning
- **05:15** — Wake. The hotel won't be serving breakfast — eat a konbini onigiri + the wagashi from yesterday. Coffee from the convenience store. Yes, really.
- **05:30** — Walk/taxi to **Inari Station** (JR Nara Line from Kyoto Station, 5 min, ¥150). First train from Kyoto runs ~05:00; the JR Nara Line runs every 10–15 min. Taxi from central Kyoto is ~¥1,800 and gets you to the gate by 05:50.
- **06:00** — **Fushimi Inari Taisha**. Gates open 24 hours, the shrine itself is always accessible. Sunrise is **~06:24**. At 06:00 you'll have the lower torii corridors practically to yourself — maybe a handful of joggers and a few photographers. By 07:30 it's full. By 09:00 it's unwalkable.
  - **Senbon Torii** (the famous double-tunnel section, ~10 min uphill from the main shrine): solo photos possible until ~06:45.
  - Continue up to the **Yotsutsuji intersection** (~30–40 min from base) — viewpoint over Kyoto with kitsune (fox) statues. Most people stop here, sensibly.
  - **Full summit loop** (~2 hr round trip, 233m peak): the upper sections are emptier and the small inari shrines tucked into the cedar forest are the real depth. The torii are denser at the bottom and thin out at the top, so expectations adjust accordingly. Decision point at Yotsutsuji based on legs.
- **08:30** — Back at Inari Station. **Breakfast / coffee:** **Vermillion Cafe espresso bar** (a 2-min walk from the back of the shrine — north exit, Fukakusa side) — small, run by an Australian-Japanese couple, proper espresso and a deck over a pond. Opens 09:00. If too early, hold out for Tofuku-ji.
- **09:30** — JR Nara Line **two stops back** to **Tofukuji Station**. 3 min, ¥150. Walk 10 min to **Tofuku-ji**.
- **09:45** — **Tofuku-ji** (¥1,000 combined Tsutenkyo + Hojo gardens). Real talk on foliage: Tofuku-ji's maple valley peaks **late Nov, mid-Nov in a warm year**. Nov 4 will be **mostly green with first yellow/orange tips**. The Tsutenkyo bridge view will be a *green* maple sea, not red. **The architecture is the point even green:**
  - The **Hojo (abbot's quarters) gardens** designed by Mirei Shigemori in 1939 — four sides, four different gardens, the grid-checkerboard moss-and-stone one is the most famous modernist Zen garden in Japan. This is *not foliage-dependent.*
  - The **Sanmon gate** — oldest surviving Zen temple gate in Japan (1425). Massive.
  - The **Tsutenkyo bridge** — even green, it's a corridor through a maple canopy and the architecture is striking.
  Allow 90 min.

### Afternoon
- **11:30** — Walk 5 min to **Funda-in (Sesshu-ji)** — Tofuku-ji subtemple, ¥500, almost no one goes. Has a Sesshu-designed garden (1467) and a tea house. Quiet, beautiful, takes 20 min.
- **12:15** — Lunch back near central Kyoto: take JR to Kyoto Station, walk or subway to **Karasuma-Oike** area. **Honke Tagoto** (Sanjo arcade) — traditional Kyoto soba and *nishin-soba* (herring soba), a Kyoto specialty. Open 11:00–20:00, closed Wed — *wait, closed Wed.* So instead: **Kaneyo** (Pontocho, *unagi* specialist since 1866, lunch sets ¥3,500–6,000) for the off-path-but-historic option. Or **Yoshikawa Inn** for tempura lunch (¥6,000+, reserve via hotel).
- **14:00–16:30** — **Wander block: Nakagyo / Karasuma backstreets.** This is downtown Kyoto away from the temples. Anchor it loosely around:
  - **Ippodo Tea** (Teramachi) — Kyoto's most serious traditional tea merchant, since 1717. In-store tea bar where you can taste 3 grades of matcha or sencha (¥800–2,000). This is the tea pick, not Tsujiri.
  - **Nishiki Market** edge (skip the deep end with the lines — walk through the eastern third where the local shops are).
  - **Sou Sou** (the modern Kyoto textile brand) — fun for both of you.
  - **Inoda Coffee Honten** (Sakaimachi/Sanjo) — the kissaten. 1940 origin, dark wood, white-jacketed staff, "the Arabian Pearl" blend. Order an egg sandwich for ritual completeness. Open from 07:00 daily.
  - Sonja's J-beauty Kyoto specifics: **Kazurasei** (Gion-shijo) for camellia-oil hair products — a 160-year-old shop, not a tourist trap; the *Tsubaki-yu* hair oil is the signature. Also **Suehiro Honten** if essential oils are her thing.
- **16:30** — Coffee: **Weekenders Coffee Tominokoji** — micro-roastery hidden in a parking lot off Tominokoji street, single-origin pour-over. 7.5–18:00, closed Wed (again — verify; if closed, **Kurasu Kyoto** near the station).

### Evening
- **17:30** — **Yasaka Shrine** at dusk → walk south through **Maruyama Park** → return to Gion via Ishibei-koji (yes, again — it's that good and you didn't see it lit up Day 11).
- **18:30** — Dinner: **Gion Sasaki** (counter kaiseki, 2 Michelin) is the prestige pick but requires booking *months* ahead via hotel concierge, and the night will include sake pairings you'd be skipping. **Substitute:** **Giro Giro Hitoshina** — punk-rock kaiseki, counter seating, ¥4,500 set menu, ages-down/streetwise approach. Kawaramachi area, reservation strongly recommended (via Tabelog or hotel). Closed irregular — verify. **Or:** **Roan Kikunoi** if you want classical kaiseki without going for the flagship — Michelin 1-star, courses around ¥15,000, more accessible booking window.
  Either kaiseki: ask up front to skip the sake pairing; they will substitute *genmaicha* or non-alcoholic *amazake*. They're used to it.
- **20:30** — Walk through **Pontocho** alley once on the way back — atmosphere only, lanterns lit, very narrow, very photogenic, lots of doors you're not walking through. 10 minutes is enough.
- **21:00** — Optional last stop: **Ippodo's** in-store tea bar closes at 18:00 so it's done; instead, **% Arabica Fujii Daimaru** stays open later for a final coffee, or just head back.

## Rain plan B
Fushimi Inari is doable in light rain (the gate corridors are continuous wood-roof) but loses atmosphere. If heavy: cut the summit, do Senbon Torii only (~30 min), then move the Tofuku-ji visit forward, add **Sanjusangen-do** (1001 wooden Kannon statues, indoor, breathtaking, ¥600). Skip Maruyama in evening, do the Pontocho walk under umbrella — even better in rain.

## Photography golden hour
- **Sunrise (~06:24):** You're in Senbon Torii at the moment first light starts filtering through the cedars on the upper levels. This is the shot.
- **Sunset (~17:00):** Yasaka Shrine entrance lanterns coming on. Or Pontocho around 17:30 once the lanterns light but sky still has color.
- Tofuku-ji Tsutenkyo bridge from the lower-level walking platform looking up — best in soft cloud cover.

- **Roan Kikunoi** (alt) — 2–3 weeks ahead.

## Off-path notes
- **Funda-in** (Sesshu-ji subtemple) sees maybe 5% of Tofuku-ji's visitor count and is more beautiful per square meter. Don't skip.
- **Ippodo Tea's** ground-floor tea bar (Kaboku) is the best tea-tasting in Kyoto for an in-person experience — explicit instructions, real ceremony framing, no Instagram queue. Sit at the counter, not the salon.
- If you want a *kissaten* depth pick beyond Inoda: **Smart Coffee** on Teramachi (since 1932) does French toast that's been the same recipe for 90 years; **Tsukimochiya Naomasa** is a wagashi/coffee combination kissaten that's older than most countries.
- The "skip Pontocho" instinct from a no-alcohol angle is right for stopping in but wrong for walking through — the lane is genuinely one of the prettiest 200m stretches in Japan. Walk it; don't enter anything.

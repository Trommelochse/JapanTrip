# Day 8 — Friday, Oct 30 2026 — Hakone → Takayama

> **Theme:** Travel day with a payoff — the Hida Wide View train through the gorges, arriving in Takayama for old-town twilight.
> **Weather (typical):** 6–15°C in Takayama, dry. Mornings noticeably colder than Hakone. Long sleeves + a real layer.
> **Walking:** Low–medium (≈4 km, mostly evening in Sanmachi).

## Schedule

### Morning
- **07:30** — Last morning soak at the Hakone ryokan, then breakfast. Don't rush this; the train story below has slack.
- **09:00** — **Hand bags to the ryokan front desk for Takkyubin (Yamato Transport)** forwarding direct to the Takayama ryokan. Address card in Japanese pre-printed from your booking confirmation. Pay ~¥2,000/bag. Travel with daypacks only — see `practical-takayama.md`.
- **09:45** — Hakone Tozan train / bus down to Odawara · 40–50 min depending on origin (Gora vs. Sengokuhara).
- **10:43** — **Shinkansen Hikari 503 Odawara → Nagoya** · ~1h 40min · ¥10,560 unreserved-ish; reserve seats D/E (right side) for Mt. Fuji views on a clear morning. Book via SmartEX app a week ahead.
- **12:25** — Arrive Nagoya. You have ~50 minutes. Don't wander — head straight to the JR side, platforms 10/11.
- **12:40** — Lunch: **Yabaton Matsuzakaya Nagoya Honten** (basement of Matsuzakaya dept store, 5-min walk from station via underground) is the Nagoya miso-katsu institution — but if you're tight on time, grab an **ekiben at Gransta Nagoya**, specifically the *Hida-gyu Shigure* bento or the *Miso-katsu Sand* from Yabaton's station kiosk. Eat on the train.

### Afternoon
- **13:18** — **Hida 9 Wide View Ltd Express Nagoya → Takayama** · 2h 17min · ¥6,140 (incl. limited express fee, reserved seat) · **Reserved seats only on this leg during foliage season — book exactly 1 month out, i.e. Sept 30. Sit on the right (seats A) heading north for the Hida River gorge views from Mino-Ota onward.**
- **15:35** — Arrive **Takayama Station**. Tourist info office inside the station — grab the English bus map and the Higashiyama walking map.
- **15:50** — Walk to ryokan (most are 10–15 min east, across the Miyagawa) · drop daypacks, confirm Takkyubin bags arrived, change into something walkable. **Don't put yukata on yet** — you want street shoes for the evening.
- **16:30–19:00** — **Wander block: Sanmachi-suji old town.** Three parallel streets (Ichinomachi, Ninomachi, Sannomachi) of dark cedar merchant houses, latticed windows, hanging cedar balls (*sugitama*) marking the sake breweries. **The breweries are an architectural and visual experience — kura warehouses, hand-painted signage, lacquered counters — not a tasting stop for us.** Pop into one or two just to see the interiors (Niki Shuzō and Funasaka Shuzō have the most photogenic frontages). Combine with: **Fujii Bijutsu Mingeikan** (folk art collection in a 1879 merchant house, ¥700, until 17:00) if you arrive by 16:00; otherwise just stroll. As dusk falls (~17:10 sunset), the wooden buildings are lit by lanterns and the day-tripper crowds collapse — this is the magic hour, and the reason we arrive Friday evening.
- **17:30** — Snack: **Sangawaya Honten** · 43 Kamisannomachi · grilled mitarashi dango, soy-sauce style (not sweet — the Takayama distinction). 9:00–17:30, closed Tue. ¥120/skewer. Eat standing, watching them grill over charcoal.

### Evening
- **18:45** — Dinner: **Hidatakayama Kyoya** · 1-77 Ōshinmachi (just east of the old town, ~6 min from Sanmachi) · 19th-century restored farmhouse with exposed beams and irori hearth seating. The signature is **Hida beef *hōba-yaki*** — beef, miso, leek, shiitake grilled on a magnolia leaf over a tabletop brazier. The 2,000-yen set is the entry point; the 4,500-yen course is the right move for a foliage-trip arrival night. **Reserve via email or Tabelog 1–2 weeks ahead** — Friday in foliage season fills. Hours 11:00–14:30 / 17:00–21:00, closed Tue.
- **20:30** — Walk back along the Miyagawa. Stone bridges, dark river, no neon. This is the contrast you came to Takayama for.
- **21:00** — Ryokan onsen (most Takayama ryokans have one indoor and one open-air rotenburo). Sleep early — tomorrow is a market morning.

## Rain plan B
If it's raining at arrival, skip the Sanmachi wander after 30 minutes and go indoors: **Takayama Yatai Kaikan** (festival float exhibition hall, ¥1,000, until 17:00) — the elaborately lacquered Edo-period festival floats with mechanical karakuri dolls. Genuinely impressive even if museum-skeptical.

## Photography golden hour
- **Sunset (~17:10):** Lower Sannomachi-dōri, looking south. The lanterns at the breweries come on around 16:45.
- **Specific shot:** Nakabashi Bridge (red, low-arched) over the Miyagawa from the Yasugawa-dōri side, with the Higashiyama temple hills behind. Best ~10 min after sunset, blue hour.

## Reservations needed for this day
- **Hida 9 Wide View seats** — book exactly 1 month out (Sept 30) via SmartEX, JR Central English site, or at any JR Midori-no-Madoguchi counter. See practical file for nuances.
- **Hidatakayama Kyoya** — 1–2 weeks ahead. Tabelog reservation page or phone +81 577-34-7660 (concierge can help).

## Off-path notes
- Skip **Hida Kokubun-ji** if you're tired — it's fine but you'll see better temples on Day 9's Higashiyama walk. The 1,200-year-old ginkgo in its courtyard is the only must-see element, and you can swing by in passing.
- **Ohitsu Gohan Shirakawa** (Sannomachi) — a tiny rice-bowl spot run by a couple, ranked highly on Tabelog (3.55+). Don't go tonight — note for a Day 9 lunch fallback if Suzuya queues are long.
- The cedar balls (*sugitama*) hanging at brewery entrances turn from green to brown over the year — fresh green ones signal the new season's product is ready. By Oct 30, most will still be green-bronze. Worth knowing what you're looking at.

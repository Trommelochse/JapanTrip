# Day 2 — Saturday, October 24, 2026 — Tokyo

> **Theme:** Old Tokyo on foot, then a digital art bath at night.
> **Weather (typical):** 14–20°C, dry. Saturday in Yanaka draws Tokyoites, not foreign tour groups — the vibe is local-weekend, not theme-park.
> **Walking:** High — 8–10 km across the day.

## Schedule

### Morning

- **08:30** — Light breakfast at the hotel or a konbini coffee. Save appetite for Yanaka Ginza.
- **09:30** — Yamanote / Chiyoda line to **Nippori** (east exit) or **Sendagi**.
- **09:45–11:00** — **Yanaka Cemetery + back lanes**. Walk south through Yanaka Cemetery (the cherry-tree main path, oddly serene for a graveyard), then drift west into the lanes around Yanaka 3-chome. This is one of the few Tokyo neighborhoods that wasn't firebombed flat in 1945, which is why the wooden houses and crooked street pattern still exist. Stop at **SCAI The Bathhouse** (a 200-year-old converted sento now a contemporary art gallery — Yanaka 6-1-23, Tue–Sat 12:00–18:00; check what's on before going, exhibitions rotate with long install gaps).
- **11:15** — Coffee: **Kayaba Coffee** · 6-1-29 Yanaka, Taito · a kissaten in a 1938 machiya, lovingly restored. Egg sandwich + a black coffee. **Closed Mondays — Saturday is open 08:00–18:00**, queues form by 11:30 so go early or commit to 15 min wait. Cash and cards both fine.
- **12:15** — **Lunch: Hantei** · 2-12-15 Nezu · a 100+ year-old wooden building serving kushiage (skewer fry) lunch sets. Lunch from ¥2,800. **Closed Mondays.** Reserve a day or two ahead via phone or your hotel concierge — Saturday lunch books up. The room is the point as much as the food.

### Afternoon

- **13:45–16:30** — **Wander block: Yanaka → Nezu → Sendagi (the "Yanesen" triangle).** 2h45 of pure drifting.
  - **Yanaka Ginza shopping street** — 200m of small shops. Niku no Suzuki's menchi-katsu is the legendary snack (¥250, eat it standing). Yanaka Shippoya's cat-tail doughnuts are a Sonja thing. Sendou senbei (1913) for rice crackers.
  - **Nezu Shrine** (free, open till 17:00) — vermillion mini-torii tunnel, smaller and quieter than Fushimi's. 10 min walk south of Sendagi.
  - **Optional split:** Sonja → Yanaka Ginza food crawl + Isetatsu (1864 chiyogami paper shop, 2-18-9 Yanaka); Clemens → SCAI follow-up + the secondhand bookshops on Shinobazu-dori around Nezu (Kashiwaya, Ouraido).
- **16:30** — Coffee / wagashi break: **Kanekichien** (tea shop, Yanaka 3-chome) for hojicha + a single wagashi if it's open, or back to Kayaba for an afternoon coffee. Alternative: **Yanaka Coffee** roastery on Yanaka Ginza for a takeaway pourover.

### Evening — teamLab Planets

- **17:30** — Train: Sendagi → Shin-Toyosu via Yurakucho line (change at Kasuga or Iidabashi → Yurakucho line; ~45 min, ¥320). teamLab Planets is 2 min walk from Shin-Toyosu Station (Yurikamome line).
- **18:30–20:30** — **teamLab Planets TOKYO** · 6-1-16 Toyosu, Koto · ¥3,800–4,800 weekend adult, 15-min timed entry, **MUST book ahead via teamlabplanets.dmm.com**. You wade barefoot through water in one room and lie in another while koi swim around you in projected light. ~90 min inside + 30 min for lockers/shoes/changing. Wear shorts or roll-up-able pants. Saturday evening 19:00 slot is the sweet spot — bus-tour crowds gone, dinner shift cleared.
- **Note:** Planets, **not Borderless**. Borderless reopened at Azabudai Hills in Feb 2024 and is also excellent, but Planets is the more physical/water-based one and the route.md anchor specifies it.

### Dinner — late, near Toyosu

- **21:00 — Dinner: Tonkatsu Tonki branch in LaLaport Toyosu** — no, scratch that, the original is in Meguro. Instead: **Tsukishima Monja Street** is two stops away on the Yurakucho line. Monjayaki (Tokyo's pan-fried batter dish, a thing locals eat, foreigners rarely) at one of the Nishinaka-dori counters. **Iroha Honten** (3-4-5 Tsukishima) is the old-school pick — open till 22:00, cash preferred. Or if you're spent, eat at **Toyosu Senkyaku Banrai** market complex right next to Planets — 2F has a row of small sushi and donburi counters open till 21:00.
- **22:30** — Back to hotel. Long day.

## Rain plan B
Yanaka still works in rain — the lanes are narrow, every block has a covered shop. Swap Yanaka Cemetery for an extra hour at Kayaba and an indoor museum: **Asakura Museum of Sculpture** (Yanaka 7-18-10, ¥500, closed Mon/Thu, Sat OK), a sculptor's preserved house-and-garden, is the rainy-day Yanaka pick. teamLab Planets is entirely indoors and arguably better in rain.

## Photography golden hour
- **Sunrise (~05:55):** Skip.
- **Sunset (~17:00):** Nezu Shrine torii arcade in last light if you time it (just before lighting up at 17:00). Otherwise Yanaka Ginza's "Yuyake Dandan" stairs at the west end face exactly west and are named for the sunset view down them.
- Shot suggestion: low angle through the Nezu mini-torii arcade, no people.

## Reservations needed for this day
- **teamLab Planets** — book 1–2 weeks ahead minimum for a Saturday evening slot (sells out). Buy direct from teamlabplanets.dmm.com.
- **Hantei** — call 1–2 days ahead for Saturday lunch (your hotel can do this).

## Off-path notes
- **Skip Senso-ji / Asakusa entirely on this trip.** The draft included it; it's overrun. Yanaka does the "old Tokyo" register better and with one-tenth the foreigners.
- **Hagiso** (3-10-25 Yanaka) — a former apartment building turned art-café-gallery-mini-hotel. Coffee + a pastry on the ground floor if Kayaba's queue is too long. Their breakfast set is also great if you redesign Day 5 morning.
- **Don't bother with Skytree.** It's far, you'll see it from teamLab Planets' approach anyway, and Tokyo views are better from Shibuya Sky on Day 3 if you want them.
- **Yanaka cat note:** real stray cats, lots of them, especially around the cemetery and Yanaka 5-chome lanes. They're a known local thing.

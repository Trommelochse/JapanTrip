# Hotels — Tokyo

For nights **Oct 23 – Oct 28, 2026** (5 nights). All prices checked **17 May 2026** via Booking.com / hotel direct sites for a twin-bed room, two adults, those dates. Treat them as ballpark — Tokyo rates move ±15% week-to-week as Oct/Nov inventory thins.

## Neighborhood call

For 5 nights with a mix of east (Yanaka, Tsukiji, Kiyosumi) and west (Harajuku, Shibuya) days, the best base is something **on the Yamanote loop between Tokyo and Ueno**, OR **Ebisu / Shibuya** if leaning west-Tokyo lifestyle, OR **Yanaka / Ueno** if leaning shitamachi-mood. Avoid Roppongi (wrong energy for this trip), avoid the Shinjuku station megacomplex (transit hell to reach Yanaka and Kiyosumi).

My default recommendation: **Yanaka/Ueno or Marunouchi/Nihonbashi side.** It puts you 10 min from Tokyo Station (for the Day 4 Kamakura departure) and 15 min from Shibuya.

## Budget — €60–110 / night

**Hotel Graphy Nezu** · Nezu/Yanaka · [booking.com/hotel/jp/graphy-nezu](https://www.booking.com/hotel/jp/graphy-nezu.html)
- Why this pick: A converted residential building turned design-hostel-hotel right in the heart of Nezu — you literally walk to Day 2's morning route. Cafe/lounge on the ground floor, mix of dorms and private twin rooms. Indie-Tokyo character, not chain blandness.
- Room type for two: **Deluxe Twin (private bath)** · ¥14,000–17,000 (~€85–105) as of 17 May 2026.
- Catch: Rooms are small (~14 m²), bathrooms compact. No on-site concierge. Some shared common spaces. Books up — 5-night Oct twin availability already partially gone; lock now.

**Sawanoya Ryokan** · Yanaka · [sawanoya.com](https://www.sawanoya.com)
- Why this pick: A real family-run ryokan in old Tokyo, traditional tatami + futon, communal cypress baths. The kind of place foreign travelers used to find in *Lonely Planet* in 1995 and somehow it still exists. Steps from Day 2's Yanaka route.
- Room type for two: **Japanese-style twin futon room with private toilet** · ¥13,000–16,000 (~€80–100) per room.
- Catch: Futons on tatami (Sonja or Clemens may prefer beds for 5 nights of jet-lag recovery). No elevator. Limited English. Book direct via their site; they often don't show on the big aggregators.

## Mid — €130–230 / night

**NOHGA Hotel Ueno Tokyo** · Ueno (5 min walk to Nezu / Yanaka) · [nohgahotel.com/ueno](https://www.nohgahotel.com/ueno/en/)
- Why this pick: 130-room lifestyle hotel positioned exactly between west-Tokyo Yamanote ease and east-Tokyo shitamachi character. Bikes for guest use (Tokyobike — you can ride into Yanaka). Strong design hotel for the price. 3 min walk to Ueno station (Yamanote + Keisei to Narita as a bonus).
- Room type for two: **Spacious Twin (~26 m²)** · ¥32,000–38,000 (~€195–230) as of 17 May 2026 for late-Oct weekday nights, slightly higher on Sat 24 Oct.
- Catch: Wedged into a slightly anonymous block — the surroundings aren't picture-postcard. Restaurants on-site are good but not the move when Yanaka is 10 min away.

**Hotel Niwa Tokyo** · Kanda / Chiyoda · [hotelniwa.jp/en](https://www.hotelniwa.jp/en/)
- Why this pick: Boutique business hotel with a strong wabi-sabi design language — Japanese garden in the lobby, tatami corner in rooms, well above its price grade. Excellent location: 6 min walk Suidobashi, 12 min walk Tokyo Station, central for Kamakura day trip and Tsukiji.
- Room type for two: **Comfort Twin (~22 m²)** · ¥28,000–34,000 (~€170–210) as of 17 May 2026.
- Catch: Rooms vary in size — book "Signature Twin" if budget allows, the Comfort Twins are functional but not photo-worthy.

## Splurge — €280+ / night

**Aman Tokyo** · Otemachi · [aman.com/hotels/aman-tokyo](https://www.aman.com/hotels/aman-tokyo)
- Why this pick: The current high-water mark of Tokyo luxury. Top 6 floors of the Otemachi Tower with a 30m central atrium, all stone-wood-shoji. The 33rd-floor pool is one of the great hotel pools in the world. Pure Japanese-minimal aesthetic, not Western-hotel-with-Japanese-accents. Walking distance to Tokyo Station for Kamakura.
- Room type for two: **Deluxe Twin (71 m²)** · ¥220,000–280,000 (~€1,330–1,700) per night as of 17 May 2026.
- Catch: It's a serious financial commitment. Save the splurge for the Hakone ryokan as planned — but if "the Tokyo arrival should feel like *we made it*" matters, this is the most beautiful place to land. Otemachi at night is dead quiet, which some love and some find sterile.

**HOSHINOYA Tokyo** · Otemachi · [hoshinoresorts.com/en/hotels/hoshinoyatokyo](https://hoshinoresorts.com/en/hotels/hoshinoyatokyo/)
- Why this pick: A vertical *ryokan* — yes, in central Tokyo. Tatami floors throughout the building, you take off shoes at the entrance. Top-floor open-air **onsen** (rooftop, real hot-spring water piped up — one of the only natural-onsen hotels in central Tokyo). A preview of Hakone two days later, at half the per-night cost.
- Room type for two: **Sakura Twin or Yuri Twin (~50 m²)** · ¥90,000–130,000 (~€545–790) per night as of 17 May 2026.
- Catch: Some find the "ryokan in a tower" concept gimmicky; either it works for you or it doesn't. The route.md ryokan splurge is Hakone, so doing Hoshinoya Tokyo doubles the ryokan register — fine if you love it, redundant if you don't.

## My pick

**Hotel Graphy Nezu** for 5 nights — Deluxe Twin with private bath, ~€85–105/night. Tokyo is a city you barely sleep in: dawn at Tsukiji, late-evening Yanaka, teamLab past 21:00. A small but characterful room in Nezu — steps from Day 2's walk and a 10-min ride into Shibuya — is the right shape for a city stay where the room is just where you charge phones. Total for 5 nights: ~€425–525 for both. Leaves clean budget room for the Hakone ryokan.

If the Graphy is fully booked when you get there: **Sawanoya Ryokan** (same neighborhood, futons on tatami, similar price) is the equivalent fallback.

**Skip** the mid and splurge tiers above for this trip. They're all good hotels but the per-night premium goes toward room comfort you won't use. Save the money for one solid mid-tier Hakone ryokan.

**Avoid** Park Hyatt Shinjuku (still closed for renovation in 2026), Trunk Yoyogi-Koen (wrong neighborhood for this itinerary's east/west split), and Roppongi anything (off-axis from your routes).

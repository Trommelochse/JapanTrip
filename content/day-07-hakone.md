# Day 7 — Thu, Oct 29, 2026 — Hakone

> **Theme:** One real outing (art + volcano + lake), bookended by the ryokan. Second kaiseki is the close.
> **Weather (typical):** 9–17°C, slightly higher rain risk than Day 6 (~35%). November-clear skies start arriving around now — Fuji visibility on Thursdays in late October averages ~50%.
> **Walking:** Medium. ~6–8 km, mostly on flat museum paths and short transfers.

## Schedule

### Morning
- **06:30** — **Dawn onsen.** The off-path moment of the entire Hakone stay. Most guests sleep until 8:00 breakfast; the rotenburo at 6:30 is empty, the steam is thick, and the first light comes through the cedars. Bring nothing but the small towel.
- **08:00** — **Ryokan breakfast** in the dining room or in-room. Grilled fish (typically aji or salmon), tamagoyaki, tofu, nori, pickles, miso soup, rice, fruit. Pour the cold mugicha or hot hojicha — both are normal here.
- **09:30** — Check the day's first decision: **is the ropeway running?** Check [Hakone Ropeway status](https://www.hakoneropeway.co.jp/en/) at breakfast. If wind >30 m/s or volcanic activity, it's closed — flip immediately to the rain/wind plan below.
- **09:50** — Walk to Gora Station (most splurge ryokans run a free shuttle). Take the **Hakone Tozan Cable Car** up to Sounzan (10 min), then the **Hakone Ropeway** to Owakudani (8 min, covered by Free Pass).
- **10:15** — **Owakudani** · the active volcanic valley · 45–60 min. Steam vents, sulfur smell, kuro-tamago black eggs (¥500 for 5 — one each is plenty; the legend says +7 years per egg, but the egg itself is just a hard-boiled egg). On a clear day, Fuji sits directly behind you to the west as you walk up the viewing path. **Going early matters:** by 11:30 the daytrip tour buses arrive from Tokyo and the deck fills.

### Afternoon
- **11:30** — Continue the ropeway to **Togendai** (15 min) on Lake Ashi. The descent gives the iconic Fuji-over-the-lake view; **sit on the right side of the gondola** going down for the best angle.
- **11:50** — **Hakone Sightseeing Cruise** (the "pirate ship") · Togendai → Moto-Hakone · 30 min · covered by Free Pass · departures roughly every 30–40 min. **Stand or sit on the right side of the upper deck** going from Togendai toward Moto-Hakone — Fuji stays on the starboard for most of the crossing. The torii of Hakone Shrine appears in the lake as you approach Moto-Hakone — it's the postcard shot.
- **12:30** — Light lunch at Moto-Hakone: **Bakery & Table Hakone** (lakefront, soup-and-bread sets ¥1,500, foot-bath terrace seats — yes, you eat with your feet in an onsen) **or** soba at **Hatsuhana Honten** if you can wait for a table (Hakone-Yumoto only; skip if you're staying lakeside).
- **13:30** — **Wander block** along the lakefront and the Ancient Cedar Avenue (Kyu-Kaido Sugi-namiki) — a 500m path of 400-year-old cedars planted by the Tokugawa shogunate. ~45 minutes of quiet flat walking, almost no tourists once you're 200m off the boat dock. Sonja → lakefront cafes and shrine; Clemens → cedar avenue and Hakone Checkpoint museum (small Edo-period customs station, ¥500, 20 min, genuinely interesting).
- **14:45** — Bus back via the Hakone Tozan bus (covered by Free Pass) to **Gora area for the Hakone Open-Air Museum.** Plan: arrive **15:30, stay until 16:30 close.** This is the off-path timing trick — the museum empties dramatically in the last 90 minutes as tour groups leave to catch trains back to Tokyo. Picasso Pavilion (300+ works, mostly ceramics — surprising and excellent), Henry Moore sculptures, Niki de Saint Phalle's Miss Black Power, and the symphonic sculpture stained-glass tower. ¥2,000, 9:00–17:00 (last entry 16:30).
- **16:45** — **Snack/coffee:** the museum's own café is fine, but **NARAYA CAFE** (5 min from Miyanoshita Station, 1 cable car stop down from Gora) is the better call if you can time it — repurposed ryokan storehouse, foot bath on the terrace, hojicha latte and onsen manju (¥600). Closed Wednesdays — fine for Thursday. Last orders 17:00.

### Evening
- **18:00** — Return to ryokan. Pre-dinner onsen #3.
- **19:00** — **Kaiseki dinner #2 (SPLURGE).** Same kitchen, completely different menu by design — the chef will have planned around the fact you're staying two nights. Expect: a different hassun, a different sashimi composition (often a more theatrical presentation on night two — a fish carved tableside, or a clay-pot crab course), a different grilled course (fish on night one usually means wagyu on night two, or vice versa), and a more elaborate final rice course (matsutake gohan or kuri-gohan is the late-October flex). **No alcohol pairing — request hojicha or genmaicha alongside.** Ask the okami (or your room attendant) about the seasonal motif at the start; they will explain the menu's logic if you show interest.
- **21:30** — Final long onsen soak. If the ryokan offers a private kashikiri-buro (reservable family bath), book it for 22:00 — the public baths get quiet, but a 45-minute private soak at the end of Day 7 is the move.
- **23:00** — Pack for Takayama tomorrow morning. Small bag only — the main suitcases are already at the next hotel.

## Rain plan B
**Critical for Hakone.** If the ropeway is closed (wind, volcanic activity, or heavy rain), the day pivots:
- **Morning:** Hakone Tozan train to **Chokoku-no-Mori** (Open-Air Museum) at 9:00 opening — large covered Picasso pavilion + outdoor sculptures with umbrellas provided + heated indoor café. 2 hours.
- **Midday:** Bus to **Pola Museum of Art** (Sengokuhara area) · ¥2,200 · 9:00–17:00. All indoor, climate-controlled glass-and-mirror building in a beech forest. Monet, Renoir, Cézanne, Van Gogh, plus rotating modern shows. Honestly one of Japan's best small museum collections — and far less touristed than the Open-Air. 2 hours inside, plus the 40-min covered forest nature trail if rain breaks.
- **Afternoon:** **Lalique Museum** in Sengokuhara (¥1,500) — Art Nouveau glass collection with the actual Orient Express dining car you can have tea inside (reservation needed for the carriage, ¥2,500). Or back to the ryokan early for an extra-long onsen afternoon.
- Skip Lake Ashi entirely on a rain day — the cruise runs but the Fuji view is gone, and the open-air decks are miserable.

## Photography golden hour
- **Sunrise (~06:00):** From the ryokan onsen (no camera — just look) or the balcony.
- **Owakudani (~10:30):** Fuji-with-steam-vents in the foreground. Best in the morning before haze builds.
- **Lake Ashi torii (~12:00):** From the cruise, right side, as you approach Moto-Hakone. The classic shot.
- **Sunset (~17:00):** The Open-Air Museum's outdoor sculpture garden in low slanting light is genuinely beautiful and underphotographed.

## Reservations needed for this day
- **Open-Air Museum** — no reservation, walk-in. Skip the online "skip the line" upsells.
- **Pola Museum** — no reservation needed; if you go on rain day, arriving at opening avoids the one busy hour around 11:00.
- **Kaiseki #2** — already locked when you booked the ryokan. The kitchen confirms timing at breakfast.

## Off-path notes
- **Dawn onsen at 06:30** is the single best, unphotographable, unrepeatable moment of the Hakone stay. Don't sleep through it.
- **Pola Museum > Open-Air Museum** on the "genuine art experience" axis. The Open-Air is photogenic and a good outing; the Pola is a real curated collection in a beautiful building, and almost no foreign tour groups go. If you only do one museum, do the Pola — and structure the day to fit it in mid-morning before the ropeway, with the Open-Air dropped.
- **Naraya Cafe** at Miyanoshita is the local favorite for a foot-bath-and-hojicha pause. Closes 17:00, closed Wednesdays.
- **Stay for last entry, not first opening, at the Open-Air Museum.** It empties from 15:30 as Tokyo daytrippers leave. The Picasso Pavilion at 16:15 with a handful of other people in it is a different experience.

# Day 1 — Friday, October 23, 2026 — Tokyo

> **Theme:** Land, breathe, eat something simple, sleep.
> **Weather (typical):** 14–20°C, mostly dry. Late-October Tokyo is the most reliable weather window of the year.
> **Walking:** Low — under 3 km. This is a recovery day.

## Schedule

### Evening (arrival)

- **~18:00** — Land HND. Clear immigration / collect bags. HND is faster and calmer than NRT and your hotel is ~45 min away on transit.
- **18:30–19:00** — Buy Suica at the Keikyu or Monorail ticket counter (welcome Suica is fine; can also add to phone via Apple Wallet on the spot — Suica works on every train, bus, vending machine, and konbini you'll touch this trip). Pick up an eSIM if you didn't pre-load one (Ubigi or Airalo activated on arrival is easiest).
- **19:00** — **Keikyu Airport Line** Haneda → Shinagawa (~18 min, ¥330), then transfer to your hotel district. If staying west/Shibuya side, stay on Keikyu through to Sengakuji and switch to Toei Asakusa Line. If staying east/Yanaka or Ueno, take the Yamanote from Shinagawa. Last Keikyu out of HND is 23:48; last Monorail 23:44 — you've got buffer.
- **20:00** — Hotel check-in. Drop bags. Splash water on your face.

### Dinner — keep it close

- **20:30 — Dinner: a neighborhood standing-soba or tonkatsu place within 5 min walk of the hotel.** Specific picks depending on hotel area:
  - **Near Shibuya / Ebisu:** **Ebisu Yokocho** (alley off Ebisu-dori) for grilled-fish teishoku at one of the corner counter spots — pick the one with the most salarymen in it, not the one with English chalkboards. Open till late.
  - **Near Shinjuku east:** **Tonkatsu Suzuya** (Kabukicho) — old-school katsu joint, tonkatsu chazuke is the move. Open till 22:00.
  - **Near Yanaka / Ueno:** **Hantei** does kushiage but books up — for tonight just hit any standing-soba inside Ueno or Nippori station. Yudetaro-ish chains are honest, fast, ¥600.
  - Fallback that always works: konbini run (Lawson > 7-Eleven for sandwiches; 7-Eleven > Lawson for everything else). Onigiri + oden + a tamago sando is a legitimate first-night dinner in Japan.

- **22:00** — In bed. Resist the urge to "push through." Tomorrow is real.

## Wander block

- **21:00–21:45** — **Mini-wander around the hotel block.** Whatever neighborhood you're in, do a slow loop. Find the closest konbini, the nearest station exit, the vending-machine corner. This is reconnaissance, not sightseeing. If you're in Shibuya / Ebisu it doubles as your first taste of dense Tokyo nightscape; if you're in Yanaka it's monastically quiet by 21:00 and that's the point.

## Rain plan B
Doesn't matter — you're inside trains and a restaurant. If it's pouring on arrival, take a taxi from Shinagawa (¥3,000–5,000 to most central hotels) rather than dragging luggage through changeovers. Worth it for one ride.

## Photography golden hour
- **Sunset (~16:55):** You'll miss it; you're still in the air or in line at immigration. Tokyo's neon is the compensation.
- Skip the camera tonight. Tomorrow.

## Off-path notes
- **Don't try to "see" anything tonight.** No Shibuya Crossing, no Skytree. Every minute you push tonight is borrowed from tomorrow's enjoyment. The draft suggested Shibuya Crossing as a first-night activity — ignore that; you'll have all of Day 3 in Shibuya and you'll see it on the walk to dinner if you're staying nearby anyway.
- **Konbini onigiri rule:** the triangular ones with seaweed sealed separately (look for the plastic-tab opening instructions) are the ones to get. Salmon (sake) and umeboshi are the safe defaults.
- **Tap water is fine** — no need to buy bottled. Tokyo's tap is among the cleanest of any megacity.
- **Trash:** there are basically no public bins. Carry your konbini wrappers back to the hotel. Get used to this now.

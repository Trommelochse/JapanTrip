# Day 4 — Monday, October 26, 2026 — Kamakura day trip (sleep Tokyo)

> **Theme:** Coastal temple town day; one of Japan's most underrated foliage starts, and the contrast against Tokyo's pace is the whole point.
> **Weather (typical):** 13–19°C, dry. Sea breeze adds 1–2°C of perceived chill — bring a light layer.
> **Walking:** High — 9–11 km, mild hills at Hase-dera and Hokoku-ji.

## Schedule

### Morning — Tokyo to Kamakura

- **07:30** — Breakfast: konbini or a station bakery. Quick.
- **08:15** — **JR Yokosuka Line from Tokyo Station** (track 1–4, underground level) direct to **Kamakura**. ~57 min, ¥950 one-way, no reservation needed. Sit on the right for the brief glimpse of Tokyo Bay. (From Shinagawa or Shimbashi works too — same train passes through.) Monday morning is quieter than weekend trains by an order of magnitude.
- **09:15** — Arrive Kamakura. Transfer to the **Enoden (Enoshima Electric Railway)** at the adjacent platform — buy the **Enoden 1-day pass (¥800)** if you'll hop on/off, or single tickets (¥200 each).
- **09:30** — Enoden two stops to **Hase**.

### Hase loop

- **09:40–11:00** — **Hase-dera** · 3-11-2 Hase · ¥400 · open 08:00–16:30 (Oct–Feb). Hillside temple, famous 9m wooden Kannon, but the actual draw is the **upper observation terrace overlooking Sagami Bay** and the moss gardens on the way up. Climb to the top, sit. Crowd is moderate Monday morning, not packed. Allocate 1h15.
- **11:15–12:00** — **Kotoku-in (Great Buddha)** · 4-2-28 Hase · ¥300, +¥50 to crawl inside the statue · open 08:00–17:30 (Oct–Mar 08:00–17:00). 5 min walk uphill from Hase-dera. The 13.4m bronze Amida Buddha has sat under open sky since the 1498 tsunami took out its hall — the open-air composition is the unusual thing. 45 min is enough; don't over-stay.
- **12:15 — Lunch: Kamakura Matsubara-an** · 4-10-3 Hase · soba in a renovated 80-year-old house, 3 min walk from Hase station. Duck seiro is the order. ~¥1,800. Open 11:30–14:30 lunch, **Monday is open** (check on the day to be safe — they close one weekday but it rotates). Reservations help but walk-ins around 12:00–12:15 usually OK.
- **Backup lunch:** **Kamakura Kitahashi** (Hase area, soba in a 100+ year-old wooden building), 11:00–15:00. Or **Hase Shokudo** for shirasu (whitebait) rice bowls — the local specialty.

### Afternoon

- **13:45** — Enoden back one stop to **Yuigahama** OR walk 10 min south.
- **13:45–14:30** — **Yuigahama Beach.** 10–15 min of just standing on the sand looking at the Pacific. Late October the beach is empty. The vibe is the destination, not anything to "do."
- **14:30** — Enoden to **Kamakura Station** (or walk 25 min north along the Wakamiya-oji avenue, the old shrine approach — actually preferable if energy is OK; passes through the original 13th-century street grid).

### Wander block — Komachi-dori + side lanes

- **15:00–17:00** — **Wander block: Komachi-dori shopping street + the parallel Wakamiya-oji avenue.** 2 hours. Komachi-dori is 360m of small shops — wagashi, senbei, lacquerware, hatsumode-style street snacks (croquettes, dango). It's a tourist street but a *Japanese*-tourist street, not a foreign-influencer set. Sonja → snack + small ceramics crawl; Clemens → duck off into the side lanes (Onarimachi, behind Tsurugaoka Hachimangu) for the quieter shops and Komyo-ji-style hidden temples.
  - **Stops to look for:** **Toshimaya** (1-5-1 Komachi) for hato-sablé (dove-shaped shortbread, the Kamakura souvenir), **Saryo Tsuruoka** for matcha + warabi-mochi sit-down, **Hotokeya** for handmade wagashi.
  - **Tsurugaoka Hachimangu Shrine** at the top of Wakamiya-oji — free, the historical anchor of the city, open till sunset. Walk through, don't make it a 90-min stop.

### Optional add-on (if energy and time allow)

- **15:30 alternative — Hokoku-ji ("Bamboo Temple")** · 2-7-4 Jomyoji · ¥400 + ¥600 for matcha · 09:00–16:00 (last matcha ~15:30) · 10 min by bus from Kamakura station east side. Smaller, calmer than Arashiyama's bamboo grove and you can sit with matcha looking into the bamboo. If interested, swap this for half the Komachi-dori wander.

### Evening — back to Tokyo

- **17:30** — JR Yokosuka Line Kamakura → Tokyo. Trains every 10–15 min. Get a green-car upgrade (¥780 weekday) for a reserved 2nd-floor seat if tired.
- **18:45** — Back in central Tokyo.
- **19:30 — Dinner near hotel.** Keep it close after a long day. Suggestions by hotel area:
  - **Around Ebisu / Shibuya:** **Afuri** — no wait, that's on the no-list. Instead **Menya Musashi** Shibuya for tsukemen, or **Tonkatsu Maisen Aoyama** if not done yesterday. Better: **Tsujihan** (Nihonbashi or Ginza branch) for kaisen-don if you can swing it.
  - **Around Tokyo Station / Marunouchi:** **Tsujihan Nihonbashi** (3-1-15 Nihonbashi) for the kaisen-don and dashi-rice combo, ¥1,400. Lines but quick service.
  - **Easy backup:** depachika at Isetan Shinjuku or Takashimaya Nihonbashi — pre-cooked Japanese deli food at international-airline-business-class level. Eat in the hotel room.

## Rain plan B
Hase-dera and Kotoku-in both work in rain (Kannon is indoors, Buddha is outdoors but doesn't care). The hillside paths get slippery — wear grippy shoes. Skip Yuigahama Beach if pouring; substitute **Kamakura National Treasure Museum** (¥700, in Tsurugaoka Hachimangu grounds) or extend Hokoku-ji matcha. **Avoid Kamakura entirely** only if heavy rain — the train ride is the same regardless and the temples shine in mist.

## Photography golden hour
- **Sunrise (~05:55):** Skip — you're on a train.
- **Sunset (~16:55):** Yuigahama Beach faces south but the western end gets golden side-light. Or shoot Kotoku-in's Great Buddha silhouetted against the sky from the south — closing time is 17:00, push it close.
- Shot suggestion: Hase-dera's upper terrace at 11:00 looking down across the rooftops to Sagami Bay — the diagonal of pine-tile-ocean is the iconic Kamakura frame.

## Reservations needed for this day
- None mandatory.
- **Kamakura Matsubara-an** — optional, helpful for a Monday lunch certainty.

## Off-path notes
- **Skip Enoshima.** It's a possible add-on on the Enoden but it's a half-day in itself and would crush the wander. Save Enoshima for a future trip.
- **Komyo-ji** (6-17-19 Zaimokuza), south of Yuigahama — 1243 temple, huge wooden Sanmon gate, almost no foreigners, free. A 10-min walk from the beach. If you want one "secret" Kamakura temple: this one.
- **Hato-sablé note for Sonja:** Toshimaya's dove-shaped shortbread is the gift souvenir of Kamakura. Get a small box (~¥1,000) for yourselves; bigger boxes for souvenirs back home.
- **Don't waste time on Kamakura's Engaku-ji + Kencho-ji northern temple loop on this trip** — they're great but you're doing Kyoto temples for days. Hase + Komachi-dori + a bamboo or beach moment is the right Kamakura cut.

# Hotels — Takayama

For nights **Oct 30, Oct 31, Nov 1 2026 (3 nights)** — Friday, Saturday, Sunday during peak Hida-valley foliage week. **This is among the hardest blocks in the trip to book.** Sat-Sun overlapping peak alpine foliage = the upper-tier ryokans sell out 4–6 months in advance. By mid-May 2026 (today), the splurge picks are already showing limited availability. **Book within a week.**

Prices verified 2026-05-17 via Booking.com, Agoda, and Japanese Guest Houses for the actual Oct 30 / Oct 31 / Nov 1 dates. All include the standard ryokan kaiseki dinner + Japanese breakfast unless noted.

## Budget — €110–160 / night

**Sumiyoshi Ryokan** · Honmachi (Miyagawa riverfront, 1 min from morning market) · [booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/jp/sumiyoshi-ryokan.html)
- **Why this pick:** Early-1900s traditional Takayama townhouse turned 20-room family-run ryokan. River-view tatami rooms, small indoor public bath (no minerals — not a real onsen, but a real bath), excellent breakfast that gets 9.6/10 from Booking guests. The location is the budget pick's secret weapon — you walk out the door into the morning market.
- **Room type for two:** Japanese-style twin futon room, river view · ¥22,000 (~€135) breakfast-only, Oct 30 + 31 + Nov 1 as of 2026-05-17. Dinner not offered — eat out (which suits this stop anyway).
- **Catch:** No private bath in rooms. Shared bath has set hours. Limited English at check-in but doable. Cash preferred for incidentals.

**Tabist Hotel Around Takayama** · Hanaoka-machi (5 min walk west of station) · [tabisthotel.com](https://tabisthotel.com/around-takayama/) / Booking.com
- **Why this pick:** Modern budget hotel chain; Western beds, private bathrooms, decent on-site bath (not a true onsen). Useful fallback if the ryokan idea isn't landing for two of the three nights and you want one night to just sleep on a mattress.
- **Room type for two:** Western twin · ¥14,500 (~€88) room-only, available all three nights as of 2026-05-17.
- **Catch:** It's a business hotel. Zero atmosphere. Use it as a safety net, not a primary pick.

## Mid — €230–350 / night

**Honjin Hiranoya Kachoan** · 1-34 Honmachi (heart of old town, 3 min from Sanmachi) · [booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/jp/honjin-hiranoya-kachoan.html)
- **Why this pick:** The mid-tier sweet spot in Takayama. Proper ryokan with two onsen baths fed from Okuhida hot-spring water (real mineral onsen, unusual for in-town Takayama — most have to truck water in), 24 tatami rooms, kaiseki dinner built around Hida beef and Hida-valley vegetables. Location is unbeatable — you can walk to the morning market, Sanmachi, and Takayama Jinya in under 5 minutes each. Free station pickup.
- **Room type for two:** Standard Japanese-style room with private toilet, 10 tatami mats, kaiseki dinner + breakfast · ¥48,780 per room (~€295) Oct 31, slightly less Oct 30 and Nov 1. As of 2026-05-17, all 3 nights still available but only 2–3 room types remain.
- **Catch:** Building is older and floors creak. Some rooms share corridor toilets (filter for "private toilet" when booking). The mid-tier rooms don't have in-room onsen — you'll use the (excellent) shared baths.

**Oyado Koto no Yume** · Hanasato-machi (2 min walk from station) · [booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/jp/oyado-koto-no-yume.html)
- **Why this pick:** A modernized ryokan with thoughtful contemporary design layered over traditional bones. Two onsen baths plus four private bookable baths (no extra charge — book at check-in). Kaiseki is well-reviewed and they're flexible about course pacing. Best for travelers who want ryokan ritual without creaky floors. Station-adjacent location matters on the 07:50 Shirakawa-go bus morning.
- **Room type for two:** Japanese-style twin with private toilet + sink · ¥35,703 (~€216) room+meals, Oct 30 and Nov 1; Oct 31 climbs to ¥41,000 (~€248) as of 2026-05-17.
- **Catch:** It's the most polished ryokan on this list and consequently the least *Edo-period* feeling. If you want creaking timber and irori, choose Hiranoya. If you want a great hot bath and competent service, this is it.

## Splurge — €400+ / night

**Wanosato** · Nyūkawa-cho Iwai (20 min from town, free shuttle from station) · [ryokancollection.com](https://www.ryokancollection.com/ryokan/wanosato/)
- **Why this pick:** A 160-year-old gasshō-zukuri farmhouse, relocated and reconfigured as an 8-room ultra-traditional ryokan, set on its own forested river plot. Private cypress and bedrock baths. Kaiseki served in-room with hearth-grilled river fish and Hida beef. This is the closest you can get on this trip to the Hakone-splurge register *for Takayama* — and crucially, it's the gasshō-zukuri architecture that you're spending Nov 1 photographing, except here you sleep in it. **It is, however, away from town** — which means after-dinner Sanmachi wandering is out (the shuttle has fixed times). Better for one of the three nights, not all three.
- **Room type for two:** Standard tatami suite, kaiseki + breakfast · ¥54,150 per person (~€326 pp = ~€652 for two), so the room runs **€600–700/night**. Oct 30 and Nov 1 showing availability as of 2026-05-17; Oct 31 already at "1 room left" warning.
- **Catch:** The location is the trade. No walking back from Sanmachi at 21:30. The 07:50 bus to Shirakawa-go means a 06:30 shuttle to town — they'll do it, but coordinate ahead.

**Hidatei Hanaougi** · Hodaka-machi (10 min by free shuttle from station) · [selected-ryokan.com](https://selected-ryokan.com/ryokan/hidatei-hanaougi_hida-takayama_gifu_nagoya-chubu-hokuriku.html)
- **Why this pick:** 48 rooms, of which 25 have private open-air onsen baths on the balcony. Hodaka-machi location is suburban (not in the old town) but the property compensates with proper Hirayu-onsen water (trucked from the source, but the real thing — mineral content tests verify). Kaiseki here is strong and the room-with-rotenburo experience is the appeal.
- **Room type for two:** Japanese-style room with private open-air bath · from ¥39,727 pp (~€480/night for two), Oct 30 + Nov 1 available, Oct 31 limited as of 2026-05-17.
- **Catch:** Aesthetic is more 1990s-luxury-ryokan than purist; some rooms feel dated. The private rotenburo is the reason to book — confirm room type explicitly.

## My pick

**Three nights at Sumiyoshi Ryokan** — Japanese-style twin futon room, river view, breakfast only, ~€135/night. ~€405 for both across 3 nights.

The reasoning: Hakone is the splurge moment of the trip — that's locked. Takayama's role is immersive depth, not another splurge. Sumiyoshi gives you the actual Takayama-ryokan experience (tatami, futon, riverside, family-run, 1900s townhouse) at less than half what Hiranoya costs, in a better location — you walk out the front door into the Miyagawa morning market. The 9.6/10 breakfast is the big-meal-of-the-day; dinner you eat out (which suits this stop because Hida beef on Sanmachi *is* the experience). No real onsen in-house — but the public bath is genuinely good and the trip already has Hakone's onsen anchor.

If Sumiyoshi shows no availability when you go to book: **Tabist Hotel Around Takayama** is the western-bed safety net at ~€88/night, or move up to **Honjin Hiranoya Kachoan** (€295/night) only if you want the actual in-house mineral onsen + kaiseki ritual.

**Book within the week.** Saturday Oct 31 is the hardest single night in the entire trip, even at the budget tier.

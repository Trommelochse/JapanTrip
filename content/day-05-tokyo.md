# Day 5 — Tuesday, October 27, 2026 — Tokyo

> **Theme:** Market breakfast at dawn, then a slow craft-and-coffee afternoon in Kiyosumi. Last Tokyo day before Hakone.
> **Weather (typical):** 13–19°C, dry. Likely the clearest day of the Tokyo week — late October Tokyo tends to peak crisp on weekday mornings.
> **Walking:** Medium — 7–9 km, no hills.

## Schedule

### Pre-dawn / Morning — Tsukiji

- **06:00** — Up. Hotel coffee.
- **06:45** — Train to **Tsukijishijo** (Oedo line) or **Tsukiji** (Hibiya line). Note: the wholesale market moved to Toyosu in 2018; the **Tsukiji Outer Market is the food street that remained** — vendors, restaurants, knives, dried goods, tamagoyaki, fresh oysters. Tuesday is a fully active trading day (some shops close Wednesdays and Sundays — Tuesday avoids both).
- **07:00–09:00** — **Tsukiji Outer Market breakfast crawl.**
  - **Tamagoyaki:** **Yamacho** or **Shouro** — both have whole skewers of sweet rolled omelet for ¥150–300. Yamacho is sweeter/caramelized, Shouro more dashi-forward. Try both.
  - **Sushi breakfast:** skip the famous queues (Sushi Dai at Toyosu, Sushizanmai chain). Try **Sushi Bun** if the queue is short, or **Tsukiji Itadori Bekkan** for a chirashi set ¥2,500–3,500. The off-path move is **Iwasa Sushi** (4-10-14 Tsukiji) — small, eight-seat counter, no English menu, regulars-and-trade clientele, opens 07:30. Point at the ¥3,800 nigiri set. They take Suica.
  - **Other stops:** Marutoyo for tamago sandwich, Tsukiji Sanokiya for grilled scallop on the street, dried bonito at Akimoto.
  - Honestly: don't plan stop-by-stop. Get there at 07:00, drift for two hours, eat 6 small things.
- **09:15** — Walk 10 min east to **Tsukiji Hongan-ji** — a 1934 Indian-styled Buddhist temple, looks nothing like anything else in Tokyo. Free, opens 06:00. 15 min stop. The temple's first-floor café does an unusual "18-item breakfast set" (¥1,800) modeled on 18 Buddhist precepts if you didn't fill up at the market — Tuesdays open.

### Late morning transition — Kiyosumi

- **10:00** — Walk to **Tsukiji station** (Hibiya line), or it's a 25 min walk across the Sumida → Kiyosumi-Shirakawa. Train is easier: Hibiya line one stop to Hatchobori, transfer to **Keiyo line** one stop to Hatchobori (no — easier path: Toei Oedo line from Tsukijishijo direct to Kiyosumi-Shirakawa, 4 stops, ~10 min, ¥220).
- **10:30** — Arrive Kiyosumi-Shirakawa. Whole different Tokyo — warehouses, low-rise, almost no foreign tourists.

### Kiyosumi: coffee + craft + garden

- **10:30–11:15** — **Kiyosumi Teien (Kiyosumi Garden)** · 3-3-9 Kiyosumi · ¥150 · open 09:00–17:00. Classic Meiji-era stroll garden with stepping stones over the central pond. 45 min loop. Quiet on a Tuesday morning. The contrast against the wholesale-market intensity is the point of pairing them.
- **11:30 — Coffee #1: Blue Bottle Coffee Kiyosumi-Shirakawa Flagship** · 1-4-8 Hirano · the first Blue Bottle in Japan (2015), in a renovated warehouse. The architecture is a Schemata Architects project, the pour-over bar is the original. ¥600 for a single-origin. Open 08:00–19:00, Tuesday yes.
- **12:15 — Coffee #2 (yes, two): ARiSE Coffee Roasters** · 1-13-8 Hirano · the neighborhood's pre-Blue-Bottle pioneer, a ten-seat counter and tiny roaster. Order the espresso shot or filter of the day. ¥500. Open 09:30–18:00, **closed Mondays — Tuesday OK**. The owner is the local third-wave godfather; this is the more-soulful pick of the two.

### Lunch

- **13:00 — Lunch: Fukagawa Meshi at Fukagawajuku** · 2-1-7 Tomioka · ¥1,500–2,200 · Fukagawa-meshi is the historical neighborhood dish — clams steamed over rice with miso (Bukkake style) or simmered (Nukabuna style). A working-class Edo-period sailor's meal. Fukagawajuku is one of the few places still doing the traditional version. Tuesday open, lunch 11:30–14:30. 10 min walk south of Kiyosumi-Shirakawa station.
- **Backup:** **Iseya** (also Fukagawa area) for unagi.

### Afternoon — wander + craft

- **14:30–17:00** — **Wander block: Kiyosumi-Shirakawa + Fukagawa craft / coffee / gallery loop.** 2.5 hours.
  - **The coffee continues:** **Allpress Espresso Tokyo Roastery** (3-7-2 Hirano), **Fukadaso Café** (a converted apartment building also housing small craft studios — pottery, glass, paper), **Cream of the Crop** (a small-batch roaster).
  - **Craft:** **Fukadaso** (1-9-7 Hirano) is the killer find — a 1928 timber apartment building turned mini-complex of artisan studios, gallery, and café. Open Thu–Sun typically, but check — **Tuesday may be closed.** If closed: **TOKYObike** (Hirano, 3-7-2) for the indie bike brand's flagship + showroom; **Onogi Kobo** (small ceramics studio if open).
  - **Optional split:** Sonja → MOT (Museum of Contemporary Art Tokyo, see below); Clemens → coffee + design wander.
  - **MOT (Museum of Contemporary Art Tokyo)** · 4-1-1 Miyoshi · 10 min walk from Kiyosumi-Shirakawa, ¥1,400 base + special exhibitions extra · open 10:00–18:00, **closed Mondays — Tuesday OK**. Yanagi Sori, Yayoi Kusama, contemporary Japanese design. 1.5–2h easily.

- **17:00 — Coffee or wagashi closer:** **Iyemon Salon Kyoto** if doing Marunouchi later; in Kiyosumi: **Tsukino Tonari** or back to ARiSE for a closer.

### Evening

- **18:30 — Dinner: Kanda Yabu Soba** · 2-10 Kanda Awajicho · 14th-generation soba house, founded 1880. Wooden-house interior, the seiro soba is the order, ¥800. Open 11:30–20:00, closed Wed. Tuesday yes. 20 min by Hanzomon/Marunouchi line from Kiyosumi-Shirakawa. The seiro + tempura set is ¥1,800 — the move.
- **Off-path alternative:** **Komaki Syokudo** (Akihabara) for shojin (Buddhist temple) ryori at a counter for ~¥1,800 — vegan, refined, almost no foreigners.
- **Pre-Hakone wind-down:** Skip late-night anything. Pack tonight. Romancecar departs early-ish from Shinjuku tomorrow.
- **21:30** — Hotel. Pack.

## Rain plan B
Tsukiji works in rain (everything is under awnings). Kiyosumi: skip the garden, double MOT time, take a taxi between coffee shops if needed. Both halves of the day function indoor-heavy in rain.

## Photography golden hour
- **Sunrise (~05:55):** Walking through Tsukiji's outer alleys as smoke rises off grills — the most photographable Tokyo morning.
- **Sunset (~16:55):** Kiyosumi Garden's stepping-stones over still water with last light. Or the Sumida riverbank near Kiyosumi (10 min walk west) facing the Skytree.
- Shot suggestion: low angle along Tsukiji's Marunaka-dori with grill-smoke + lanterns lit, ~07:30.

## Off-path notes
- **Toyosu market (the actual wholesale market)** is a possible alternative — tuna auction observation deck, 05:30. It's earlier, more sterile, but the "real" market. The trade-off is no Tsukiji food street experience. Outer Market gives more flavor for less effort. Pick one, not both.
- **Skip teamLab Borderless** even though it's Tokyo's other big "must-do." You did Planets. Two teamLabs in five days is overkill.
- **Skip Akihabara** unless either of you specifically cares about anime/electronics. The draft listed it; it doesn't fit this trip's culture-old/culture-new register.
- **Hagi-cha** — try roasted hojicha tea at Yanaka Sasanoyuki or any decent café this week as a counterpoint to the matcha you'll drink in Kyoto.
- **Packing reminder:** consolidate to one small overnight bag per person for Hakone — the ryokan has limited room space. Tomorrow morning (Day 6) at the front desk, **takkyubin (forward) your big suitcases from the Tokyo hotel directly to your Takayama hotel** (~¥2,000/bag, ~2-day transit, bags arrive Takayama by Day 8 evening). Then a second forwarding leg Takayama → Kyoto on Day 11. See practical-tokyo.md and practical-takayama.md for the full takkyubin plan.

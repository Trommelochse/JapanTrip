# Day 6 — Wed, Oct 28, 2026 — Tokyo → Hakone

> **Theme:** Slow exit from Tokyo, slip into the mountains, settle into the splurge. The night is the event.
> **Weather (typical):** 9–17°C in Hakone (cooler than Tokyo by ~3–4°C), partly cloudy, ~30% rain probability. Tail end of typhoon season — check the Japan Meteorological Agency forecast 48h out.
> **Walking:** Low. ~3–5 km total. Mostly transit, then a quiet afternoon at the ryokan.

## Schedule

### Morning
- **08:30** — Slow breakfast at the Tokyo hotel or a kissaten near Shinjuku. No rush — the Romancecar reservation is the only fixed point.
- **09:30** — Drop forwarding luggage at the Tokyo hotel front desk via Yamato Takkyubin. Send the big suitcases **directly to the Takayama hotel** (Day 8 destination), skipping Hakone. Travel to the ryokan with one small overnight bag each. ~¥2,000/bag, arrives in 2 days. See `practical-hakone.md`.
- **10:30** — Walk to Shinjuku Station, Odakyu line ticket gates (south side, ground level — well signposted in English).
- **11:00** — **Odakyu Romancecar "Hakone" or "Super Hakone"** · Shinjuku → Hakone-Yumoto · ~85 min · ¥2,470 total (¥1,280 base + ¥1,200 limited express, digital "Ticketless" via EMot is ¥50 cheaper). Reserved seats only. Book ~3 weeks in advance via [e-Romancecar](https://www.web-odakyu.com/e-romancecar/?language=en). Sit on the right (seats A) for the Tanzawa mountain side after Odawara. The newer GSE trains have the front-row observation seats — worth grabbing if available.
- **12:30** — Arrive Hakone-Yumoto. Switch to the Hakone Tozan switchback train up to Gora (~40 min, covered by the Hakone Free Pass). The track zig-zags up the mountain in three reversals — windows open in shoulder season, the smell of cryptomeria forest comes through.
- **13:15** — Lunch in Gora: **Tamura Ginkatsu-tei** · Gora · tofu katsu set ¥1,800. Local institution, almost entirely a Japanese clientele, queue moves fast at lunch. Closed Wednesdays in some weeks — verify before going; backup is **Itoh Dining by Nobu** for a (pricier) wagyu set across the street.

### Afternoon
- **14:30** — **Ryokan check-in.** Most Hakone ryokans accept check-in from 14:00–15:00. Reception will explain the in-room kaiseki timing, the onsen rotation (often gender-swapped morning/evening), and the yukata sizes. Take it slow — the staff will offer wagashi and matcha or hojicha in the lounge.
- **15:30–18:00** — **Wander block (gentle version)** — this is "wander inside the ryokan and the immediate grounds." Onsen soak before dinner (1h), tatami nap, balcony with a book, second soak. Do not plan an outside activity. Hakone evenings get dark and cold fast at this altitude, and the kaiseki is the point.
- *If restless:* a 20-minute walk from Gora area takes you to the Hakone Museum of Art's moss garden — small, contemplative, ¥1,300, last entry 16:00. It's the second-oldest museum in Hakone, and almost empty on a Wednesday.

### Evening
- **18:30** — **Kaiseki dinner #1 (SPLURGE)** · in-room or private dining alcove (depends on ryokan — Gora Kadan serves in private dining rooms, Hakone Ginyu in-room). 8–10 courses, 2–2.5 hours. Seasonal late-October menu: matsutake mushroom dobin-mushi, sanma (Pacific saury) shioyaki, Hakone tofu, hassun platter with chestnut and persimmon, a grilled wagyu course, sashimi flight, rice with ikura or kuri, miso soup, wagashi. **No alcohol pairing.** Ask for **hojicha, sencha, or a cold barley tea (mugicha)** with the meal — the kitchen will not blink, this is a normal request from Japanese guests too. The food does the work.
- **21:00** — Final onsen soak. The outdoor rotenburo at night, with mist rising and one yellow leaf in the water, is the moment you came for.
- **22:00** — Futon already laid out. Sleep.

## Rain plan B
The day is already largely indoor. If a typhoon tail brings heavy rain on the way in: skip the Gora lunch wander and head straight to the ryokan from Hakone-Yumoto by taxi (~¥2,500–3,000 to Gora, 15–20 min). Tensui Saryo will hold your room early on request. Use the extra time for a longer pre-dinner onsen.

## Photography golden hour
- **Sunset (~17:00):** From the ryokan's rotenburo or balcony — the Hakone caldera fills with low light. Most splurge ryokans face west or south-west for exactly this reason.
- **Shot suggestion:** The yukata-on-tatami self-portrait is a cliché for a reason. Take it before dinner — the post-kaiseki version is less photogenic.

## Reservations needed for this day
- **Romancecar seats** — book 1 month before the date (seats open at 10:00 JST exactly 30 days out) via EMot app or [e-Romancecar](https://www.web-odakyu.com/e-romancecar/?language=en). October weekday departures usually have room until ~10 days out, but the 11:00 Super Hakone is the sweet spot and goes early.
- **Ryokan** — book 3–6 months ahead. See `hotels-hakone.md`.
- **Tamura Ginkatsu-tei** — no reservations, walk-in only. Aim for arrival 13:00 or after 13:30.

## Off-path notes
- **Skip the Hakone Open-Air Museum today.** It's the obvious afternoon activity from Hakone-Yumoto, but every guidebook routes Day 1 there and it's mobbed Wednesday afternoons with school groups and Tokyo daytrippers. Save it for tomorrow morning at opening.
- **Hakone Museum of Art moss garden** (not the Open-Air Museum — different place, both in Gora) is the local secret. Quiet, mossy, ~45 minutes, and the tea house overlooking the moss serves a proper matcha + wagashi for ¥1,000.
- **Don't try to "see Hakone" on arrival day.** The trap is filling the afternoon with the ropeway or Lake Ashi because you're "already there." You're paying for the ryokan. Be in the ryokan.

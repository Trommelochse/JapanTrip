# Day 12 — Tue, Nov 3, 2026 — Kyoto (Ohara escape)

> **Theme:** Culture Day escape. While central Kyoto melts down with domestic holiday crowds, you go 45 minutes north into a thatch-roof mountain hamlet where the maples turn first.
> **Weather (typical):** 7–16°C in Ohara (2–3°C cooler than central Kyoto), morning fog likely in the valley.
> **Walking:** Medium-high — ~7 km on temple paths and country lanes, some uphill.

## Why today is the escape

Nov 3 is **Culture Day** — a national holiday on a Tuesday this year, so domestic travelers extend it into a four-day weekend with the prior Saturday/Sunday. Kiyomizu, Fushimi Inari, Arashiyama bamboo grove and Kinkaku-ji will be in full meltdown. Ohara — a tiny rural valley 12 km north of Kyoto — gets a fraction of the traffic. Foliage at Ohara typically starts coloring **a full week earlier** than central Kyoto because of the elevation/temperature differential, so Nov 3 catches it in early-to-mid color. This is the deliberate move.

(We considered Kurama/Kibune as alternative — also valid, similar foliage timing, slightly more accessible but the Kibune ravine restaurants are alcohol-centric. Ohara wins for this trip.)

## Schedule

### Morning
- **08:00** — Hotel breakfast. Quick — the bus is the morning's anchor and the 09:30 departure is what fits the new schedule.
- **08:45** — Walk/subway to **Kyoto Station** (Karasuma Line if not staying within walking distance). Buy a one-day bus pass if not using ICOCA — but for Ohara, ICOCA works fine on the bus.
- **09:30** — **Kyoto Bus #17** from Kyoto Station Bus Terminal (bay C3), direct to Ohara. ~60 min, ¥630 cash or IC. Buses run every 30 min. The route runs up through Demachiyanagi and along the Takano River valley — sit on the **left** for the river view. Confirm holiday timetable at the bus terminal (Kyoto Bus runs a slightly reduced holiday schedule). **Tradeoff:** the 09:00 was the strategic sweet spot for beating Culture Day crowds; the 09:30 is 30 min behind that — expect Sanzen-in to be noticeably busier than originally planned. Still far quieter than central-Kyoto chaos today.
- **10:30** — Arrive **Ohara** bus stop. Walk uphill ~10 min through the village along the cedar-lined approach to **Sanzen-in**.
- **10:45** — **Sanzen-in** (¥700). The Yusei-en garden — moss carpet under a maple canopy, with the tiny stone *warabe-jizo* figures hiding in the green. The reading room (Shinden) sits on tatami looking out at the garden; sit. This is the slow temple. Walk the full loop including the upper precinct and the Ojo Gokuraku-in hall. Allow 60–75 min.
- **12:00** — **Hosen-in** (¥800, includes matcha + wagashi). The neighbor temple, even quieter than Sanzen-in. The *bamboo-framed view* — a 700-year-old pine framed by the open shoji — is the famous shot. They serve you matcha and a wagashi sweet while you sit on tatami looking out. This is exactly the kind of slow, ceremony-adjacent experience Clemens came for, with no alcohol framing, and the wagashi-and-tea hits Sonja's lane too.

### Afternoon
- **12:45** — Lunch: **Seryo** (just below Sanzen-in). Famous for *kyo-yasai* (Kyoto vegetable) set meals and Ohara-grown rice. Lunch sets ¥2,500–4,500. Open daily during foliage season but call to verify holiday hours. If too full, **Wappa-do** does excellent country-style *teishoku* in a thatched farmhouse setting — closes 15:00 sharp.
- **13:30–16:00** — **Wander block: Ohara village & Jakko-in side.** This is rural Japan as it actually exists, not a recreation. Walk the path through the rice paddies (harvested by now, golden stubble) across the river. **Jakko-in** (¥600) is 25 min walk on the *opposite* side of the valley from Sanzen-in — small nunnery with a tragic Heike-clan history, rebuilt after a 2000 arson fire, sits in a maple-shaded pocket. Even if you don't enter, the walk there through the village is the point. Stop at the persimmon-drying farmhouses (early November = peak hanging-persimmon season — orange globes hanging from every eave).
- **15:30** — Snack: **Café Kissako** or **Ohara Sansou** for a coffee and rest. Or pick up roasted chestnuts (*yaki-kuri*) from a roadside vendor — peak season.
- **16:15** — **Kyoto Bus #17** back to Kyoto. Last departure ~17:30 but don't push it — get the 16:15 or 16:45 to avoid the holiday-return crush. Back at Kyoto Station by ~17:30.

### Evening
- **18:30** — Dinner near the hotel — keep it low-key after a full day. Recommendation: **Mishima-tei Honten** (Sanjo/Teramachi) for *gyu-nabe* (Kyoto-style sukiyaki) in a 150-year-old building, or **Issen Yoshoku** (Gion) for the one-and-only *negi-yaki* dish if you want something casual and odd-Kyoto. Mishima-tei: ¥6,000–9,000 pp, closed Wed.
- **20:00** — **Wagashi pick-up** at **Kanshundo Higashiten** (closes 18:00, but their Kawaramachi branch goes later) for tomorrow morning's pre-dawn ride snack. Or **Demachi Futaba** *mame-mochi* if you happen to be near Demachiyanagi — but they sell out, so order ahead or accept it as a lottery.
- **20:30** — Free evening. If you have any gas left, the **Kamogawa riverside** between Sanjo and Shijo bridges is the locals' evening stroll — couples sit at neat 1-meter intervals on the bank, lit by streetlights and the buildings of Pontocho across the water. (Pontocho itself is the alley running parallel — gorgeous lantern-lit lane, mostly drinking establishments. Walk through it for atmosphere, no need to enter anywhere.)

## Rain plan B
Ohara still works in rain — it's almost better, the moss glows. Bring an umbrella. The temples are all roofed sit-and-look experiences. The only thing affected is the cross-valley walk to Jakko-in; cut that and double down on time at Sanzen-in's Shinden and Hosen-in's matcha.

## Photography golden hour
- **Sunrise (~06:24):** Skip — you need the early morning for the bus.
- **Sunset (~17:00):** You'll be on the bus back. The light hitting the persimmon eaves around 15:30 in Ohara is the better photographic window today.
- Sanzen-in's Yusei-en moss carpet is best shot midday with diffuse cloud cover — overcast is your friend. Avoid harsh noon sun.

## Reservations needed for this day
- None mandatory. **Mishima-tei** dinner — recommend booking through hotel concierge given the holiday.

## Off-path notes
- **Hosen-in's bamboo-framed view** is the photo, but the under-floor "uguisubari" (nightingale floor) at Sanzen-in is the audio. Walk the corridor slowly and listen.
- The **persimmon-hanging farmhouses** in Ohara are real working farms, not tourist exhibits. Don't trespass, but the lane between Sanzen-in approach and Jakko-in passes several. Peak hanging period mid-Oct to mid-Nov.
- If you somehow need to cut Ohara to a half day, **Sanzen-in + Hosen-in only** is the ruthless minimum and you can be back in Kyoto by 14:00. But the village walk *is* the wander block today — don't cut it.
- Bus #17 also passes **Demachiyanagi** on the way out — if you have the energy on return, hop off there and walk through the student/Kamo-delta neighborhood; this is one of Kyoto's best low-tourist wander zones.

# Foliage Timing — Oct 23 – Nov 6, 2026

Japan's autumn foliage front (*koyo*) moves north-to-south through the year, starting in the Hokkaido / northern Honshu alpine zones in early October and reaching Tokyo / Kansai by late November. Higher elevations peak earlier than the cities. This window is designed around catching **alpine foliage at peak in the Hida region (Day 10 Shirakawa-go)** while accepting that the lowland cities will be early-stage.

## What you'll see by city

| Stop | Days | Expected foliage status | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tokyo | Oct 23–27 | **None / first traces** | Gingko peaks late Nov (Meiji Jingu Gaien). Tokyo maples mid-Nov to early Dec. |
| Kamakura (day trip) | Oct 26 | **None / first traces** | Hase-dera momiji peak typically Nov 25–Dec 5. You'll see green hillsides. |
| Hakone | Oct 28–29 | **Early color at mid-elevations** | Owakudani / ropeway zone may show 30–50% turn. Lake Ashi shoreline still green-to-amber. |
| Takayama (in town) | Oct 30–31 | **Approaching peak** | Higashiyama Walking Course (temple loop east of town) is the local foliage trail; Hida-no-Sato (Folk Village) at peak. |
| Shirakawa-go | Nov 1 | **AT or just past peak** | The foliage payoff of the trip. Shiroyama viewpoint at 09:15 is the iconic frame. |
| Kyoto (city center) | Nov 2–6 | **Mostly green; first color on early-peaking spots** | Most central Kyoto temples (Eikan-do, Nanzen-ji, Tofuku-ji) peak Nov 20–30. |
| Kyoto (north / mountain) | Nov 3 (Ohara) | **Early-to-mid color** | Ohara (Sanzen-in, Hosen-in) peaks earlier than city Kyoto thanks to elevation — Nov 5–15 typically. |

## Why Kyoto is green this trip (and why that's a trade you accepted)

Kyoto's central-city momiji peaks **Nov 20–30 in average years, Nov 25–Dec 5 in warm years like 2025.** To catch it at peak you'd need to start the trip Nov 13+ — which means:
- Takayama and Shirakawa-go are past peak by then (alpine foliage moves fast)
- The Labor Thanksgiving long weekend Nov 21–23 lands directly in the Kyoto leg → genuinely unmanageable crowds
- Hakone booking inventory tightens for Sat–Sun foliage peak

The Oct 23 – Nov 6 window trades **central Kyoto foliage** for **alpine peak + low crowds + booking realism.** What you keep:
- Ohara on Day 12 is showing some color (mountain temple zone peaks earlier)
- Tofuku-ji on Day 13 — early-stage color but the architecture is the point
- Saiho-ji moss temple on Day 14 — moss is at its richest in autumn even without maple color overhead

## Reliability of this forecast

Japanese foliage forecasts get updated by JMA and Weathernews from late August onward. The 2025 season ran **10–14 days late** vs the historical average due to a warm autumn. If 2026 follows that pattern:
- Takayama / Shirakawa-go could still be at 70–80% peak rather than full peak on Nov 1 (mitigated by the elevation spread — Hida-no-Sato + Shirakawa-go are at different altitudes)
- Kyoto would actually see *more* color in early November than the historical baseline predicts
- Hakone would peak later (closer to Nov 5–10) but you're there Oct 28–29 anyway — the splurge is the ryokan, not the gardens

**Check the JMA forecast in late August 2026** for a current read on your specific dates. Adjust expectations, not plans.

## When to look at the sky vs the ground

A few foliage-specific notes worth remembering:

- **Best foliage light is mid-morning** when the sun is on the canopy but the trees aren't backlit
- **A clear day after rain** intensifies color — the leaves are washed and the contrast pops
- **Frost morning the night before** locks color in; if Nov 1 has a dawn frost in the Hida valley (likely — it usually does), the Shirakawa-go viewpoint will be peak-saturated
- **Overcast days** are actually good for the moss garden at Saiho-ji and for Ohara's mountain temple groves — diffuse light brings out the green undertones

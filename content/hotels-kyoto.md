# Hotels — Kyoto

For nights **Nov 2 – Nov 6, 2026** (4 nights — check in Nov 2 evening, check out morning of Day 15 = Nov 6).

Same room, same property all five nights. Central base — anywhere on the **Karasuma corridor from Karasuma-Oike down to Shijo**, or **Gion/Higashiyama** west of Yasaka Shrine, or **Kawaramachi/Pontocho-adjacent**. Avoid anything north of Imadegawa or south of Kyoto Station — too far from the Day-12/13/14 starts.

Mid and Splurge picks lean toward **machiya** (renovated traditional townhouses) or **design ryokan**, per the cultural-anchor brief.

Prices verified May 17, 2026 via Booking.com / official sites. November 2026 is mid-tier autumn pricing — peak weekends (Nov 21–23) are blocked or doubled, but Nov 2–7 is achievable.

## Budget — €100–160 / night

**Hotel Resol Kyoto Kawaramachi Sanjo** · Kawaramachi · [booking.com/hotel/jp/resol-kyoto-kawaramachi-sanjo.html](https://www.booking.com/hotel/jp/resol-kyoto-kawaramachi-sanjo.html)
- Why this pick: Walking distance to Nishiki, Pontocho, Gion (across the Kamogawa), and the Karasuma subway. Resol is a midmarket Japanese chain with reliable rooms, decent bathrooms, and surprisingly good breakfast. Clean, quiet, modern, not exciting — which is the point at this tier.
- Room type for two: Twin/Double · ¥21,000–28,000 (€130–175) as of May 17, 2026
- Catch: Rooms are 17–20m², small by Western standards (the Kyoto norm). No on-site restaurant of note.

**Hotel The Celestine Kyoto Gion** · Higashiyama · [celestinehotels.jp/gion](https://www.celestinehotels.jp/gion/)
- Why this pick: At the south end of Gion (near Kennin-ji), neighborhood location with stone-lantern streets at the doorstep. Boutique-business hybrid; ~120 rooms but feels smaller. Public bath on the top floor with city view — rare at this price.
- Room type for two: Twin/Double · ¥24,000–32,000 (€150–200) as of May 17, 2026
- Catch: A 10-min walk from the nearest subway (Gion-Shijo Keihan is closest). Worth it for the location.

## Mid — €180–280 / night

**Nishiyama Ryokan** · Kawaramachi · [ryokan-kyoto.com](https://www.ryokan-kyoto.com/)
- Why this pick: Family-run traditional ryokan since 1922, smack in central Kyoto. Tatami rooms with futon, optional kaiseki dinner (you'll skip — alcohol-paired), small in-house onsen-style bath. The mid-tier Kyoto ryokan that hasn't been bought by a hotel group; staff speak workable English. Walkable to everything.
- Room type for two: Standard Japanese-style room w/ private bath, **room only** (no meals) · ¥38,000–48,000 (€235–295) as of May 17, 2026
- Catch: Book room-only; the half-board pricing assumes sake pairing. Older bathrooms in the standard rooms — fine but no rain shower.

**Tassel Hotel Sanjo Shirakawa** · Higashiyama (Shirakawa Canal) · [tasselhotel.jp/kyoto-sanjo/en](https://www.tasselhotel.jp/kyoto-sanjo/en/index.html)
- Why this pick: Boutique opened 2020 on the **Shirakawa Canal** — willows and stone bridges out the window, 5-min walk to Yasaka Shrine, subway adjacent (Higashiyama T10). Rooms have tatami floors and proper showers — the design-machiya hybrid done right. Quieter half of Gion.
- Room type for two: Superior twin with canal view · ¥36,000–44,000 (€225–275) as of May 17, 2026
- Catch: Small property (~30 rooms), books out fast for autumn. No restaurant — breakfast is continental, fine but skippable.

**Machiya Residence Inn** (specific property: "Hatoba-an" or "Tomi-no-koji") · Nakagyo · [kyoto-machiya-inn.com](https://www.kyoto-machiya-inn.com/)
- Why this pick: This is a network that operates individual restored machiya as **entire-house rentals** — you get the whole 100-year-old wooden townhouse to yourselves. Two-story, narrow, hinoki bath, courtyard garden, traditional sliding doors. The most-Kyoto thing you can do as accommodation.
- Room type for two: Whole machiya, 2BR (use one), private · ¥38,000–55,000 (€235–340) as of May 17, 2026
- Catch: Self check-in via lockbox, no daily housekeeping, no staff on site. Some are 10-min from a subway. If anything goes wrong (no hot water etc), there's a phone line but not a front desk. Most-Kyoto cultural experience / least-coddled service.

## Splurge — €450+ / night

**Hoshinoya Kyoto** · Arashiyama (by boat) · [hoshinoresorts.com/en/hotels/hoshinoyakyoto](https://hoshinoresorts.com/en/hotels/hoshinoyakyoto/)
- Why this pick: Reached only by private boat upstream on the Oi River — a 25-room hidden luxury ryokan in a 100-year-old Arashiyama riverside residence. River view from every room. Star property in the Hoshino brand. The kaiseki kitchen will accommodate no-sake service.
- Room type for two: Asagiri (river-view) room · **¥110,000–160,000 (€680–990)** room-only, +¥30,000 pp dinner, as of May 17, 2026
- Catch: It's **in Arashiyama**, which means a 20-min train each way for the city-center activities — workable but not central. The brief asked for central; this is the splurge-tier exception. Three of five nights here + two in central Kyoto is a stronger pattern if you want to do this.

**Hiiragiya** · Nakagyo (Fuyacho) · [hiiragiya.co.jp/en](https://www.hiiragiya.co.jp/en/)
- Why this pick: 1818, sixth-generation family ryokan, official Registered Tangible Cultural Property. Five emperors and three prime ministers' favorite. 28 rooms, individual hinoki baths, kaiseki-by-the-room delivered on lacquerware. The benchmark traditional ryokan in central Kyoto.
- Room type for two: Standard Japanese-style room (annex side, room-only) · **¥80,000–130,000 (€500–800)** as of May 17, 2026; **classic-main-building rooms with private gardens run ¥150,000–250,000+**
- Catch: Annex rooms are newer (1970s, less character); main-building rooms are spectacular and 2–3x the price. Half-board default; ask in advance for *no-sake kaiseki* — they will handle it but need notice.

**Sowaka** · Higashiyama (Yasaka) · [sowaka.com/en](https://sowaka.com/en/)
- Why this pick: 23-room boutique opened 2019 in a restored 100-year-old former *ochaya* (teahouse) at the foot of Yasaka — the modern-design machiya done at the splurge level. Across the street from the Kawai Kanjiro neighborhood feel. Two buildings: the historic main house and a sympathetic modern annex. La Bombance restaurant on-site (Tokyo Michelin chef, kaiseki-French).
- Room type for two: Annex Suite or Main Building Standard · **¥90,000–140,000 (€560–870)** as of May 17, 2026
- Catch: La Bombance is alcohol-forward by design — skip and dine elsewhere. Sowaka is the most-fashion-forward splurge pick (younger ownership, contemporary art on the walls).

## My pick

**Hotel Resol Kyoto Kawaramachi Sanjo** for 4 nights — twin room, ~€130–175/night. ~€520–700 for both across 4 nights.

Reasoning: Kyoto days run 08:00 hotel breakfast to 21:30 night walks through Gion. The hotel is a base for sleep and showers, not a destination. Resol drops you within walking distance of Nishiki, Pontocho, Gion (across the Kamogawa), and the Karasuma subway — the four places you'll actually be — and at €130–175 the per-night savings vs. Tassel's €225–275 or Sowaka's €560+ goes much further on food, museum tickets, and the Hakone ryokan.

If the Resol shows full when you go to book: **Hotel The Celestine Kyoto Gion** (~€150–200, public bath on the top floor) is the equivalent fallback in a slightly more atmospheric location.

**Avoid** the splurge tier here — Hoshinoya's location costs you 40 min of transit every day on a stay where you've already designed routes around central-Kyoto basing, and Sowaka/Hiiragiya's kaiseki ritual is alcohol-coded by design.

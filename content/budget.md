## How to push this lower

- **Skip the Hakone splurge.** A budget Hakone ryokan with onsen (~€150–200/night, no in-room kaiseki) saves ~€300 pp but loses the trip's anchor moment. Not recommended.
- **Cut a Hakone night** (only 1 night instead of 2). Saves ~€150–200 pp. Loses the "second kaiseki + dawn onsen" that's the whole point of staying two.
- **Eat more konbini.** Lawson and 7-Eleven onigiri / sandwiches / hot food can pull food costs down to ~€20/day. Saves ~€100 pp. Some loss of cultural depth.
- **Skip teamLab.** Saves €23 pp. Real money for a single experience.

## How to push this higher (if budget loosens)

- **Mid-tier city hotels.** NOHGA Tokyo + Hiranoya Takayama + Tassel Kyoto adds ~€700–800 pp. Real comfort upgrade, no experience gain.
- **Hakone Ginyu instead of mid-tier.** Adds ~€300–400 pp. Private in-room rotenburo, in-room kaiseki.
- **One-night Aman Tokyo soft landing** for arrival night. Adds ~€600 pp. Pure indulgence.
- **Hanami private boat ride / tea ceremony / craft workshop** in Kyoto. ~€80–150 each, depending.

## Things this budget does NOT include

- Pre-trip eSIM if you want it before landing (~€15 if bought ahead)
- Yamato luggage forwarding **from Japan back to your home country** (some travelers ship souvenirs separately — ~€50/box if you do)
- Drinks (Clemens does not drink; Sonja's decision)
- Last-minute taxi for missed last trains in Tokyo / Kyoto (~€20–30 per ride if it happens)
- Photography gear, walking shoes, layers — assumed already owned

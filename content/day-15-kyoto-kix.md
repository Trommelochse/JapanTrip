# Day 15 — Fri, Nov 6, 2026 — Kyoto → KIX (depart)

> **Theme:** A slow morning food crawl through Nishiki, one last kissaten, then the Haruka to KIX. The trip ends with one perfect bowl, not a sprint.
> **Weather (typical):** 8–17°C, dry, calm.
> **Walking:** Low — ~3 km, mostly indoors / market.

## Pre-trip departure prep (do tonight, Day 14 evening)

- **Forward your big bags to KIX** via the hotel's Yamato Transport desk. Cost ~¥2,000–2,500 per bag, ~3 hours pickup → next-day-afternoon arrival at KIX baggage center. **Note:** Forwarding luggage *to* KIX requires drop-off **by ~12:00 the day before departure** to guarantee next-day same-day arrival; same-day-to-KIX is generally *not* available. If your Day 14 schedule slips, forward bags to **KIX via two-day route** instead — many hotels can configure this. Confirm with the hotel front desk on Day 13 to be safe. Alternative if you miss the window: travel with bags on the Haruka (it has dedicated oversized-bag space at car ends, requires advance seat reservation in the designated luggage area).
- **Check-in online** for your flight 24h before departure.
- Use up the cash in your wallet — Nishiki and Kyoto Station depachika are great for spending the last ¥10,000 worth on omiyage.

## Schedule

### Morning
- **08:00** — Hotel breakfast and a slow check-out (most Kyoto hotels are 11:00; if your flight is later than 14:00 you have plenty of room).
- **08:45** — Walk to **Nishiki Market** (it runs east-west from Teramachi to Takakura, just north of Shijo). Most stalls open 09:00–18:00. Going at opening means you can actually walk it without elbowing.
- **09:00–10:30** — **Wander block: Nishiki Market.** Specific picks from west (Takakura) to east (Teramachi):
  - **Aritsugu** — knife maker since 1560. They will engrave your name in kanji on a purchased knife for free. If Clemens or Sonja have ever wanted a Japanese knife, this is the moment. Tax-free for tourists.
  - **Mochitsuki Daikokuya** — fresh mochi pounded right there.
  - **Konnamonja** — soy-milk doughnuts (these are unique to Nishiki).
  - **Tako Tamago** — quail-egg-stuffed mini octopus on skewers, a Kyoto-only snack.
  - **Sawawa** — *warabi-mochi* (bracken-root jelly) tasting room.
  - **Nishiki Ichiba Tea Stand** — single-cup matcha from Uji tea.
  - **Demachi Futaba sister stall** (sometimes) — *mame-mochi*. Worth a try.
  - **Yuba (tofu skin) stalls** — Kyoto signature, get the fresh kind not the dried.
  - **Kyoto Saisai** — pickled-vegetable stalls (*Kyo-tsukemono*), especially *senmaizuki* and *shibazuke*. Great omiyage.
  - **Nishiki Tenmangu Shrine** at the eastern end (Teramachi side) — the market literally terminates in a small shrine. Stop, throw a coin, walk out.
- **10:30** — Coffee: **Inoda Coffee Honten** (5-min walk from Nishiki's western end on Sakaimachi) if you didn't make it Day 13. Get the egg sandwich + a coffee.

### Lunch
- **11:30** — Lunch: **Honke Owariya Honten** if Day 11 didn't happen / if you want a second visit; or **Menami** for one more obanzai counter (Kiyamachi, lunch teishoku ¥1,500–2,500, closed Sun); or — the lazier and equally good play — go straight to **Kyoto Station** and graze the **Porta underground** + **Isetan depachika** food halls. The Isetan basement is one of the great department-store food halls in Japan; the Day-15-final-meal there is a genuine pick, not a fallback. Get *saba-zushi*, *yuba*, a *bento*, and a final wagashi sweet box for the flight.
- **12:30** — Final wagashi pickup: **Kanshundo Kyoto Station** branch in Porta — or, if you have time, **Kameya Yoshinaga** (a 10-min walk from the station) for *kuzumochi*.

### Afternoon — Haruka to KIX
- **13:00** — At **Kyoto Station** in time to clear the Haruka platform. Trains depart **every 30 minutes** (~:16 and ~:46). 75 min ride. Fare **¥3,440** total (base ¥1,910 + Limited Express fee ¥1,530). Use ICOCA for the base fare and buy the Limited Express ticket at the machine — or buy the whole thing at the Midori-no-Madoguchi (Green Window). **Note:** the old "ICOCA & HARUKA" discount package was discontinued in 2023. The current foreign-tourist discount is the **Haruka One-Way Ticket** (¥2,200, available with passport at JR-West ticket offices) — confirm at the station; available for foreign tourists only, sometimes day-of, sometimes via online reservation.
- **13:16 or 13:46** — **Haruka** departs Kyoto.
- **~14:31 / 15:01** — Arrive **KIX Station**, walk into Terminal 1 (or take the shuttle for T2). Pick up your forwarded bags at the **KIX Hands-Free Travel** baggage counter near 1F arrivals. Check in. Allow ~2.5 hours before international departure.

**Recommended Haruka for typical flight times:**
| Flight departure | Take Haruka at | Arrive KIX |
|---|---|---|
| 16:00 | 12:16 or 12:46 | 13:31 / 14:01 |
| 17:00–17:30 | 13:46 | 15:01 |
| 18:00+ | 14:16 or 14:46 | 15:31 / 16:01 |

For a **13:00 flight** (the prompt's example): take the **09:46 or 10:16 Haruka**, arrive KIX 11:01 / 11:31. Push breakfast earlier and skip Nishiki — only fit if the flight is genuinely early afternoon.

## Rain plan B
Today is mostly indoor (market, station, train). Rain doesn't affect it. If pouring at departure, the underground passageways from most central Kyoto hotels to Kyoto Station via the subway keep you dry.

## Photography golden hour
- **Sunrise (~06:26):** Skip; you're packing.
- **Sunset (~17:00):** You're at KIX or in the air. No call here.
- Nishiki Market's overhead canopy is constantly lit but mid-morning natural overspill at the side streets is the better photo light.

## Reservations needed for this day
- **Haruka seat reservation** — book Day 14 via JR Kyoto ticket office or the JR-West Online Reservation site (free to reserve; unreserved car also available). Not strictly required but recommended on a Friday.
- **Bag forwarding to KIX** — arrange Day 13 evening / Day 14 morning at hotel front desk.

## Off-path notes
- **Aritsugu** in Nishiki is *the* knife buy in Japan. They've been doing this since 1560. Saved-for-the-end purchase that travels in checked luggage with no fuss (declare if EU customs ever asks, but it's a domestic-use chef knife, not regulated).
- **Isetan depachika** at Kyoto Station outranks most freestanding restaurants for a final meal — Kyoto specialties, picture-perfect bento, very Japanese end to the trip.
- The **Haruka** runs through nondescript southern-Kyoto landscape — don't expect scenery, this is a logistics ride.
- If you have a **late-evening flight** (20:00+), there's room for one final stop: the **Kyoto Railway Museum** near the station (20 min walk from JR Kyoto, ¥1,500, closes 17:00) for Clemens, or a final **Yojiya Cafe** sit-down for Sonja. Don't push it — the trip is done; flying tired and rushed is a worse memory than an extra hour at KIX.

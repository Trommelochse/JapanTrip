# Practical — Tokyo

Tokyo-specific notes worth surfacing before you arrive. Cross-cutting topics (packing, JR pass strategy, etiquette) live in the assembly-phase files.

## Suica / Pasmo IC card

Buy at the airport on arrival (Keikyu or Monorail counter at HND, or any JR ticket office). Welcome Suica is fine for tourists. Better: **add Suica to your iPhone/Apple Watch** via Wallet — works for ~95% of Tokyo turnstiles, vending machines, and konbini, no physical card needed, and you can top up from any major credit card. Android users: Mobile Suica via the Suica app, but it's clunkier with foreign cards; physical Welcome Suica is easier.

- Tap and go on **every train and bus in Tokyo, Kamakura, and beyond.** Hakone Tozan, Romancecar reservations not included (separate ticket).
- Load ¥3,000–5,000 to start. Top up at any station or konbini.
- One card per person — you can't tap two people through on one card.

## Neighborhood orientation (quick)

- **Yanaka / Nezu / Sendagi (Yanesen)** — old Tokyo, low-rise, shitamachi. Best for Day 2 and a chill morning anywhere. Bonus: actual Tokyo locals live here.
- **Ueno** — transit hub for east Tokyo, museums (TNM, Tokyo Met. Art Museum). Decent base.
- **Tokyo Station / Marunouchi / Nihonbashi** — central, slightly sterile, perfect for Kamakura + Shinkansen days. Best depachika at Daimaru and Takashimaya.
- **Ginza** — luxury shopping, depachika (Mitsukoshi, Matsuya), the Sony Park rebuild. Worth a 90-min stroll but no need to base here.
- **Shibuya** — youth-fashion + design, dense + loud. Shibuya Sky is the must-do here.
- **Harajuku / Omotesando** — fashion, J-beauty crawl on Day 3, architecture (Omotesando is an Olympic-gym-of-fashion-flagships street).
- **Daikanyama / Nakameguro** — design / coffee / quieter shopping. Day 3 Clemens-split candidate.
- **Shimokitazawa** — vintage / records / theater. Other Day 3 candidate.
- **Kiyosumi-Shirakawa / Fukagawa** — coffee + craft + slower east-side. Day 5 morning anchor.
- **Tsukiji** — outer market only (wholesale moved). Early-morning food, Day 5.
- **Toyosu / Odaiba** — modern reclaimed land, teamLab Planets, the actual wholesale market. Day 2 evening.

## Cash vs cards

Tokyo has gone meaningfully cards/IC-friendly in 2024–25, but **small restaurants and shrines/temples are still cash-mostly**. Carry ¥20,000 in cash at any given time. 7-Eleven ATMs accept foreign cards (Visa/Mastercard/Amex/Maestro), 24h, no fee from the Japanese side (your home bank may charge).

## Taxis

Cheap and easy compared to e.g. Paris. Flag fall is ~¥500 for the first ~1 km, then ~¥100 every 250m. Use **GO** app (Japan's Uber-equivalent; English supported, foreign cards work). After ~23:30 a late-night surcharge applies (~20%). Five-person van taxis exist for groups but you'll do fine with regular sedans.

## Last-train rule

Tokyo trains stop around 00:00–00:30. **Last train from Shibuya to Ueno on the Yamanote is ~00:20.** Plan returns by ~23:30 unless you want a ¥3,000+ taxi back. The Yamanote line is the spine you'll keep returning to — memorize that.

## Walking + station scale

Tokyo stations are *huge*. Shinjuku, Tokyo, Shibuya, Ueno can each take 10 min to walk across underground. When Google Maps says "5 min walk + 8 min train + 4 min walk," budget 25 min total. Use station **exit numbers** (e.g. "Exit B3") not "north exit" — Tokyo signage is built around numbered exits.

## Trash / etiquette quick hits

- No public trash bins. Carry your waste back to a konbini or your hotel.
- Don't eat while walking, except on designated food streets (Yanaka Ginza, Komachi-dori, Ameyoko).
- Stand on the **left** on escalators in Tokyo (the right is for walkers). This is reversed in Osaka, FYI — but irrelevant to this trip.
- IC card tap not metro-style ticket-feed: just touch and walk through.

## Luggage forwarding (Takkyubin)

Your big suitcases skip Hakone entirely. The plan:

1. **Day 6 morning (Oct 28), Tokyo hotel → Takayama hotel direct.** Hand bags to the front desk by 09:00. ~¥2,000/bag, ~2 days in transit (mountain-to-mountain). Bags wait for you at the Takayama ryokan when you arrive Day 8 evening. Travel to Hakone with one small overnight bag each.
2. **Day 11 morning (Nov 2), Takayama hotel → Kyoto hotel direct.** Same drill at the Takayama ryokan front desk. ~¥2,000/bag, next-day delivery. Travel light on the Hida #36 to Kyoto — the train is cramped.

Total cost: ~¥4,000 per bag across the trip. This is the single best money you'll spend.

# Day 11 — Mon, Nov 2, 2026 — Takayama → Kyoto

> **Theme:** Mountains to maiko. Train transition into the cultural anchor of the trip; quiet Higashiyama at golden hour.
> **Weather (typical):** 9–18°C, mostly dry, crisp evenings (sweater + light jacket).
> **Walking:** Medium — ~5 km, mostly evening on stone lanes.

## Schedule

### Morning
- **08:00** — Hotel breakfast in Takayama. Forward main bags via Yamato to the Kyoto hotel (next-day delivery; ~¥2,000/bag). Travel with carry-ons only — the Hida is cramped. **Note:** the optional last walk through Sanmachi is dropped on this iteration — the 08:34 train is tight against an 08:00 breakfast. Ask the ryokan to plate a faster set; they will, this is a normal request.
- **08:34** — Limited Express **Hida #36** from Takayama Station, direct to Kyoto (Sun/holiday timing; weekday departure 08:31). Reserved seats on the **right (D side)** — the Hida River gorge runs on that side until Gero. Bento from a Takayama Miyagawa-market stall the night before, or pick up *Hidagyu-bento* at the station kiosk. ~3h 43min, ¥8,360.
- **~12:14** — Arrive **Kyoto Station**. Drop your day bag at the hotel (most central Kyoto hotels hold luggage from noon). Late lunch in the basement of the station building.
- **13:00** — Lunch: **Shinshindo Kyoto Station** branch closed Sundays; instead head to **Hyōtei Bekkan** lunch box (¥3,500–5,500) only if you have a reservation — otherwise default to **Honke Owariya** (Sanjo, oldest soba shop in Kyoto, est. 1465). 11:00–19:00, closed Wed. Cash + cards. Get the *Hourai Soba* — five-tier soba towers.

### Afternoon
- **14:30–17:00** — **Wander block: Higashiyama backstreets.** Walk up from Yasaka Shrine through **Ishibei-koji** (a stone-paved alley most tour groups miss — it's just east of Nene-no-Michi), then onto **Nene-no-Michi** itself, **Ninenzaka**, **Sannenzaka**. The trick: do it in late afternoon when the day-trippers thin out. Pottery shops, incense at **Lisn** (modern incense bar) or the older **Kyukyodo**. Sonja: **Yojiya** flagship on Sannenzaka (yuzu hand cream, the iconic blotting paper — actually a Kyoto-original brand, not just a souvenir). Clemens: **Asahi-do** for Kiyomizu-yaki ceramics, or the **Kawai Kanjiro Memorial House** (¥900, the potter's home preserved — open until 17:00, closed Mon, so check; it's a sleeper).
- **16:30** — Coffee: **% Arabica Kyoto Higashiyama** (Yasaka Pagoda branch). Yes it has a line. The matcha latte is good. Get it to go and walk it up to the **Hokan-ji (Yasaka Pagoda)** corner — the iconic Higashiyama postcard frame. Stand back, let other photographers cycle through, take your shot at 16:45 when the light goes amber.

### Evening
- **17:30** — **Kiyomizu-dera** (¥500) — if you have the legs. It closes at 18:00 most of November but check; the wooden stage at sunset over the city is the introduction-to-Kyoto move. If you'd rather sit, skip it and head straight to dinner.
- **18:30** — Dinner: **Gion Kappa** (Higashiyama). Counter obanzai (Kyoto home-style small plates) — the off-path antidote to kaiseki theatre. ¥4,000–6,000 pp. Reserve via hotel concierge a few days out. Closed Wed. No English menu but the chef points.
  *Alt if booked out:* **Menami** (Kiyamachi, north of Sanjo) — obanzai counter, picks-from-the-row, no reservation taken, expect 30-min wait at 18:30. Closed Sun.
- **20:00** — **Hanami-koji walk** through Gion. This is the geisha-spotting stretch — Hanami-koji proper is still open to all. Stay on the main street. **Do NOT enter the side alleys** marked with the small "private road" signs; a ¥10,000 fine applies (English/Japanese/Chinese signs in place since 2024). Don't photograph maiko/geiko without consent. If you see one walking briskly between appointments, step aside. Walk north to **Shirakawa Canal** — willow-lined, the quieter half of Gion, equally beautiful and far fewer people.
- **21:30** — Free evening. Head back at your own pace via Gion-Shijo (Keihan) or Kawaramachi (Hankyu).

## Rain plan B
If it's pouring, skip Higashiyama outdoor lanes and instead: **Kyoto National Museum** Special Exhibition (closed Mon — verify; if closed, **Hosomi Museum** in Okazaki is excellent for traditional Japanese art, intimate, indoors). Then dinner unchanged.

## Photography golden hour
- **Sunrise (~06:23):** Not relevant today — you're on a train.
- **Sunset (~17:00):** Yasaka Pagoda from Yasaka Dori (the postcard frame, looking east). Be in position by 16:45.
- Ishibei-koji at 17:30 with lanterns lit — slow shutter, no flash.

## Reservations needed for this day
- **Hida #36** seat reservations — book via SmartEX or at any JR ticket office once in Japan (open 30 days ahead). Friday-after-holiday weekend means seats go fast.
- **Gion Kappa** — phone reservation 2–5 days ahead, ask hotel.

## Off-path notes
- **Kawai Kanjiro Memorial House** is the quietest cultural stop in Higashiyama — Clemens will like this more than Kiyomizu. It's the home of a 20th-c. folk-pottery master, preserved as he left it. Closes 17:00, closed Mon.
- Coffee alternative if % Arabica's line is absurd: **Kissa Ihatov** (north of Sanjo, near Yasaka), a proper kissaten with siphon coffee and zero foreigners.
- The Yamato luggage forwarding from Takayama to Kyoto is ¥2,000ish per bag — confirm same-day-noon pickup gets it there next-day; if you leave too late it lands Day 12 instead, which still works because you're not going anywhere with the bags Day 12.

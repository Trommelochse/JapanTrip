# Practical — Takayama & Shirakawa-go

City-specific logistics that don't fit neatly into day files. Read once before booking transport.

## Hida Wide View Ltd Express — booking quirks

**Trains:**
- **Day 8 (Oct 30):** Hida 9 Nagoya 13:18 → Takayama 15:35 (you reach Nagoya from Hakone via Shinkansen Hikari 503).
- **Day 11 (Nov 2):** **Hida #36 (Wide View Hida bound for Osaka)** Takayama 08:34 → Kyoto 12:14, direct service, no Nagoya transfer. ¥8,360 reserved. Runs once daily — this is the move and removes a transit risk versus the older Hida 10 + Shinkansen routing.

- **Reservation window opens exactly 1 month in advance, at 10:00 JST.** For Day 8 (Oct 30 train), the window opens **Sept 30 at 10:00 JST.** For Day 11 (Nov 2 train), Oct 2 at 10:00 JST. Set calendar reminders.
- **For foliage season, you must reserve.** The 4-car Hida sells its reserved car (~70 seats) within 2–3 days for prime mid-day runs. Unreserved cars exist but are standing-room only by Mino-Ota.
- **Sit on the right (seats A) going north** for the Hida River gorge from Mino-Ota onward (~14:30). Sit on the left going south.
- **Booking tools, in order of usefulness:**
  - **SmartEX app** (English, foreign credit cards work) — easiest, lets you reserve and ride with a QR code.
  - **JR Central English site** ([Train Reservation](https://touristpass.jp/en/)) — works, but the UI is dated.
  - **Any Midori-no-Madoguchi counter** in a major JR station — works fine if you're in Tokyo already; clerk can write out the seat assignment in English.
  - **NOT** the regular JR West site; the Hida is a JR Central train.
- **JR Pass note:** the trip skeleton explicitly skips the JR Pass. Individual tickets for the Hida (Odawara→Nagoya Shinkansen ¥10,560 + Hida 9 reserved ¥6,140 = ¥16,700, ~€100 pp) are correct.
- **If the reserved car is sold out** when you check: book a Green Car seat instead (¥3,000–4,000 extra). Green Car opens at the same time but sells slower because most foreign travelers stick to ordinary.

## Nōhi Bus to Shirakawa-go — reservation system

The bus is the only sane way to do Shirakawa-go from Takayama. There are two regulatory categories of departure, **and the foliage Sunday departures (Nov 1, 2026) are all reservation-required.**

- **Book at:** [nouhibus.co.jp](https://www.nouhibus.co.jp/route_bus/takayama-shirakawago-line-en/) (Nohi's own English page) or [Japan Bus Online](https://japanbusonline.com/en/CourseSearch/11900040002). Japan Bus Online is more reliable for foreigners; Nohi's own site occasionally rejects non-Japanese credit cards.
- **Reservation window opens 1 month in advance.** For Nov 1, open **Oct 1, 09:00 JST.**
- **Round-trip booking is recommended** — it's slightly cheaper (¥5,000 round-trip vs. ¥2,800 + ¥2,800 = ¥5,600 one-ways) and you lock the return seat.
- **Departures from Takayama (typical autumn schedule, verify on day):**
  - 07:20 — first bus, **sells out fastest** for Sundays
  - **07:50 — your target** (arrives 08:40, ahead of the day-tripper wave)
  - 08:50, 09:50, then ~hourly
- **Returns from Shirakawa-go (typical autumn):** ~14:10, 15:10, 16:10, 17:10. Last guaranteed is the 17:10 — there's sometimes a 17:50 in peak season but don't plan around it.
- **At the bus terminal:** show up 15 min early, have ticket QR ready. The terminal is inside the building next to JR Takayama Station's east exit; same building as the tourist info center.
- **Bag stowage** in the bus hold is free. Daypacks stay with you.

## Takkyubin (Yamato Transport) — incoming and outgoing

Big bags skip Hakone entirely (see practical-tokyo.md for the inbound leg). Two forwarding steps in total across the trip:

**Inbound — bags arrive in Takayama from Tokyo:**
- Bags are forwarded **Tokyo hotel → Takayama ryokan direct** on Day 6 morning, ~¥2,000/bag, ~2-day transit. They should be waiting at the Takayama ryokan front desk by Day 8 evening.
- **If bags haven't arrived by Day 9 morning:** ask the ryokan to call Yamato. Tracking numbers come with the original receipt. You should have a 24-hour overnight kit in your daypack regardless — toothbrush, change of clothes, charger, meds, camera gear.

**Outbound — Takayama → Kyoto on Day 11 morning:**
- Hand bags to the Takayama ryokan front desk by **08:00 on Day 11 (Nov 2)** for next-day delivery to the Kyoto hotel by end of day Nov 3. ¥2,000/bag.
- **Address:** the Kyoto hotel's name + address in Japanese — get this from your hotel confirmation. The ryokan front desk fills the form.
- Travel to Kyoto on the Hida #36 with daypacks only. The train is cramped and the corridor doesn't take large bags well.

## Cash, payment, connectivity

- **Cash:** Takayama is more cash-heavy than Tokyo. Sanmachi shops, dango stands, smaller restaurants (Ebisu, Suzuya, Tsuzumi) take cash only. Shirakawa-go is mostly cash-only too. **Pull ¥40,000 from the 7-Eleven ATM** inside the station before leaving Takayama on Day 8 evening or Day 9 morning.
- **IC card:** Suica/Pasmo works on the Sarubobo city bus (¥100 flat per ride). Doesn't work on the Nōhi Bus to Shirakawa-go (separate ticket).
- **eSIM coverage:** Generally fine in Takayama and along the Hida River corridor. Shirakawa-go village has good coverage; the Shiroyama viewpoint can be patchy at peak congestion.

## What to skip

- **The "sake brewery tour" tickets** sold at the tourist office. Strip these entirely (no alcohol). The breweries are worth visual entry; you don't need the tour.
- **The Takayama Festival float museum** (Yatai Kaikan) — only if rainy or you have an extra hour. It's good but not essential.
- **Hida Great Limestone Cave (Hida Daishōnyūdō)** — 30 min outside town, requires a car or expensive taxi. Skip.
- **Guided Shirakawa-go bus tours** — they leave too late (9:00+ from Takayama Bus Center) and pack you in with 40 others. Solo via Nōhi Bus is faster, cheaper, more flexible.

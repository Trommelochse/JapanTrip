# Day 3 — Sunday, October 25, 2026 — Tokyo

> **Theme:** Forest shrine in the morning, J-beauty + design wander in the afternoon, optional split for Clemens-flavored vs Sonja-flavored Tokyo.
> **Weather (typical):** 14–20°C, dry. Sunday Harajuku is busy but the energy is teenagers and locals, not tourists.
> **Walking:** High — 9–11 km.

## Schedule

### Morning

- **07:30** — Hotel coffee. Move early; Meiji Jingu is transformed by being there before the day crowd.
- **08:30** — Train to **Harajuku** (Yamanote) or **Meiji-jingumae** (Chiyoda).
- **08:45–10:30** — **Meiji Jingu** · free · opens at sunrise (≈05:45 in late Oct), closes around 17:00 in October. Enter from the south (Harajuku) torii, walk slowly through the 700,000-tree forest planted 1920 for the emperor's memorial — every tree donated from across Japan, no two species in the same cluster. Main shrine compound is plain wood, no gold, deliberately restrained. **Inner Garden (¥500)** is worth the extra ticket — Empress Shoken's iris garden, mostly quiet, has the small Kiyomasa's Well that's a low-key power-spot. Allocate full 90 min.
- **10:45** — Walk through Yoyogi Park east edge → out to Omotesando.
- **11:15** — Coffee: **The Roastery by Nozy Coffee** (5-17-13 Jingumae, on Cat Street) for a single-origin pourover. Or **Bear Pond Espresso** if you want the angular barista experience (4-29-9 Jingumae).

### Lunch

- **12:00 — Lunch: Maisen Aoyama Honten** · 4-8-5 Jingumae · the original Aoyama tonkatsu temple, in a converted public bathhouse. Tourist-known but not in the Ichiran-lane way; this is where Tokyo families come on Sundays. Roll-cutlet set ¥2,000. Open daily 11:00–22:00. No reservations, expect a 20–30 min wait at 12:30 — go at 11:45 or 14:00 to skip the worst of it.
- **Backup:** **Yoshiba Suika** in Sendagaya for chirashi if you want fish instead of fried.

### Afternoon — the split

- **13:30–17:00** — **Wander block: Harajuku → Omotesando → Shibuya, with an optional split.** This is 3.5 hours, two soft anchors, lots of free drift.

  **Together (or Sonja's track) — J-beauty crawl:**
  - **@cosme TOKYO** (1-14-27 Jingumae, Harajuku station exit) — the flagship; 750+ brands, Japanese drugstore picks and high-end on the same floor. Tax-free counter. Sonja's anchor — 60–90 min easily.
  - **Cosme Kitchen Omotesando** (Omotesando Hills basement) — natural/organic curation, harder-to-find European clean-beauty brands alongside Japanese botanicals. Smaller, more boutique-feel than @cosme.
  - **Shu Uemura Tokyo Makeup Box** (4-26-18 Jingumae) — global flagship boutique; products you can't get outside Japan, plus the cleansing-oil bar.
  - **Shiseido The Store** (Ginza, slightly off-route but worth a Day 5 detour if missed today) — for the Shiseido brand history side.
  - **Skip Don Quijote** for J-beauty depth; it's variety not curation, and the foreign-tourist density is now intense.
  - **Curveball pick:** **Beautylibrary Aoyama** (Minami-Aoyama, just south of Omotesando) — small select shop for organic/Japanese niche brands, the kind of place beauty editors source from. Quieter than @cosme.

  **Clemens's optional split — Daikanyama design loop:**
  - Train: Harajuku → Shibuya → walk 20 min OR Tokyu Toyoko one stop to Daikanyama.
  - **Daikanyama T-Site / Tsutaya Books** (17-5 Sarugakucho) — three pavilions of curated books, magazines, music. The travel and art sections are world-class. Free, open till 22:00.
  - **Okura** (20-11 Sarugakucho) — indigo-dyed Japanese workwear in an old farmhouse-style building. Even if not buying, the shop is the thing.
  - **Hillside Terrace** complex for architecture (Maki Fumihiko's masterwork, 1969–98, urbanism textbook example).
  - Coffee stop: **Saturdays NYC Daikanyama** or **Coffee Mameya Kakeru** (Aoyama, between the loops).
  - **Alternative split for Clemens:** Shimokitazawa for vintage and records — Disk Union, Flash Disc Ranch, Jet Set. 8 min from Shibuya on the Inokashira line. Denser, scrappier, more anti-design than Daikanyama. Pick based on mood.

  **Reconvene:** Shibuya, ~17:00.

### Evening

- **17:00** — **Shibuya Sky** (Shibuya Scramble Square 14-2-1 Shibuya, 46F) · ¥2,500 advance, ¥2,800 walk-up · open-air rooftop observation deck. Book the sunset slot (16:40 or 17:00 entry for late-Oct ~17:00 sunset). 360° view; on a clear day Mt Fuji to the west. Way better than Skytree — closer to the actual city, open-air, designed by Nikken Sekkei. **Book ahead** at shibuya-scramble-square.com/sky for sunset slots; they sell out by Sunday morning.
- **18:30** — Walk down to Shibuya Crossing at peak density. Three crosses on foot is the right dose.
- **19:30 — Dinner: Katsukichi Shibuya** · 2-9-1 Shibuya (Shibuya Mark City) · pork katsu specialist, less famous than Maisen, the loin-katsu set is the order. Cash + cards. Open till 22:00. Or **Kanda Yabu Soba** (if you want to bus over to Kanda, but probably too far for end-of-day).
- **Off-path dinner alternative:** **Suzunari Yokocho** alley on the Cat Street side has a row of tiny okonomiyaki and yakitori counters — pick a yakitori spot for the food (we're framing this around grilled chicken and tsukune, not drinks).
- **21:30** — Back to hotel.

## Rain plan B
Meiji Jingu in light rain is gorgeous, actually — fewer people, the moss glows. If it's pouring: skip the Inner Garden, do the main shrine fast, then collapse the wander block into Omotesando Hills + Hikarie ShinQs (Shibuya) — both covered, both have the J-beauty floors. Shibuya Sky closes the outdoor deck in heavy rain (refund/reschedule available).

## Photography golden hour
- **Sunrise (~05:50):** Meiji Jingu south torii in mist before opening crowds — but they don't open the inner gates till sunrise.
- **Sunset (~17:00):** Shibuya Sky rooftop facing west toward Fuji. The single best Tokyo sunset frame, period.
- Shot suggestion: Shibuya Sky's "edge" (the corner without rail) right after sunset for the city-lights-coming-on transition.

## Reservations needed for this day
- **Shibuya Sky** — book at least 2–3 days ahead for sunset slot (shibuya-scramble-square.com/sky).
- Restaurants — no reservations needed.

## Off-path notes
- **Skip Takeshita Street** unless Sonja specifically wants the candy-pink chaos. It's 90% foreign teenagers now and the J-beauty is better elsewhere on this route.
- **Ota Memorial Museum of Art** (1-10-10 Jingumae, ¥1,000) — small, world-class ukiyo-e collection in Harajuku. Closes 17:00. If Clemens wants a quiet 45-min break from the cosmetics crawl while Sonja shops on, this is the move.
- **Wagashi pick:** **Toraya Akasaka** flagship (Akasaka, 4-9-22) — 500-year-old wagashi house, tea + namagashi (¥1,500), exquisite shop design. Slight detour but worth it if the cosmetics crawl wraps early.
- **No Golden Gai. No Omoide Yokocho.** The draft pointed there for "drinks under train tracks." Both are now tourist sets and aren't us anyway — alcohol-free trip.

# Day 9 — Saturday, Oct 31 2026 — Takayama

> **Theme:** Slow Takayama — morning market, Hida-no-Sato in foliage, Higashiyama temple loop at dusk.
> **Weather (typical):** 4–14°C, dry. Frost possible at dawn. Bring gloves for the 7am market.
> **Walking:** Medium–high (≈8–10 km across the day, mostly flat with one moderate climb on the Higashiyama course).

## Schedule

### Morning
- **07:00** — Ryokan breakfast (most serve 7:30–9:00 — confirm at check-in if you want the earlier slot).
- **07:45** — Walk to the **Miyagawa Morning Market** along the east bank of the river · 5–10 min from most ryokans. Open 7:00–12:00. About 50 stalls — pickles (*akakabu*, the red Hida turnip), miso, *sarubobo* dolls (the faceless red baby doll, regional talisman), seasonal fruit, flowers. The energy is local-quiet; vendors are mostly farmers and their wives, not performers. **Cash.** Also walk through **Jinya-mae Market** (the smaller, often-better one in front of Takayama Jinya), 7:00–12:00. Smaller, slightly more food-stall oriented.
- **09:00** — **Takayama Jinya** · 1-5 Hachikenmachi · ¥440 · 8:45–17:00 · The only surviving Edo-period provincial government office in Japan. 60 tatami rooms, original kitchens, the torture-interrogation room (yes, really), and a giant rice storehouse out back. Allow 60–75 min. The garden is small but well-composed.
- **10:30** — Coffee: **Falò** · 32-2 Honmachi · third-wave roaster in an old machiya, opens 9:00, closed Wed. Single-origin pour-over ¥600. Sonja: the *anko-butter toast* is excellent. Clemens: ask the barista what they're roasting that week.
- **11:30** — Bus #2 (Sarubobo Bus, ¥100) or 30-min walk to **Hida-no-Sato (Hida Folk Village)**. Walk is uphill but scenic if dry.

### Afternoon
- **12:00** — Lunch at **Suzuya** · 24 Hanakawamachi · the old-town Hida beef institution. No reservations — go now while old-town is at lunch peak elsewhere. Wait will be 15–30 min Saturday. Order the **Hida beef hōba-miso teishoku** (¥3,500). 11:00–15:00 / 17:00–20:00, closed Tue. *Alternative if line is more than 40 min:* **Ohitsu Gohan Shirakawa** (Sannomachi), or **Center4 Hamburgers** for the Hida-beef burger (genuinely good, more casual).
- **13:30–16:30** — **Hida-no-Sato (Hida Folk Village)** · ¥700 · 8:30–17:00 (last entry 16:30) · This is the day's anchor. 30+ gassho-zukuri farmhouses relocated from the Shōkawa valley, set around a pond on a hillside. Open interiors with hearths burning, ironworkers and weavers demonstrating crafts (free demos, no extra fee). **By Oct 31 the maples ringing the pond are usually at 70–90% peak — vivid reds against the dark thatch is the iconic frame.** Allow 2.5–3h; this is a slow place by design.
  - **Wander block (built-in):** the village layout is loop-shaped — split up if you want. Sonja → indigo dyeing demo at building #14, woodworking. Clemens → the upper terraces (Wakayama and Nishioka houses) for foliage + Hida Alps views over the rooftops.
  - **Foliage Light-up:** through early November the village runs an evening illumination 17:30–20:30 (re-entry ticket ¥300). Worth considering as a Day 9 closer **only if Higashiyama feels too long** — see decision point below.
- **16:30** — Bus back into town. Sunset is ~17:10. **Decision point:**
  - **Option A (recommended):** Higashiyama walk at dusk (next item).
  - **Option B:** Return to Hida-no-Sato for the 17:30 illumination. Magical, but adds another bus loop and you skip the temples.

### Evening
- **17:00** — **Higashiyama Walking Course** · free · 3.5 km loop through Teramachi (temple town) and Shiroyama Park (site of the demolished Takayama Castle). 10+ small temples; the path to **Tenshō-ji** is a maple-tunnel that peaks the first week of November. **Amaterasu-ji** is the one with the blazing red grounds. Don't try to enter temples (most close by 17:00) — this is a *passage*, not a visit. Bring a small light; some side paths are dim. ~90 min at a relaxed pace. **Carry the bear bell** that Takayama tourist info hands out free — bear sightings on the wooded sections behind Shiroyama Park are uncommon but real.
- **18:45** — Dinner: **Ebisu Honten** · 60 Kamininomachi · founded 1898, Hida soba in a beautifully patinated machiya. Order the **kake soba** or the **kamonan-soba** (duck-and-leek hot soba). 11:00–17:30 (last order 17:10), closed Tue. **Goes by 17:00** — if you cut Higashiyama short for dinner here, do it. Alternative for a 19:00+ dinner: **Hyotei** (10 Suehiromachi), local-only izakaya-style spot framed around Hida-mountain vegetable dishes and *hōba-yaki* — open until 22:00, closed Wed. Ask for a window counter, not the back tatami room (smaller portions, better service).
- **20:30** — Ryokan onsen. Pack a daypack tonight for Shirakawa-go — water, layers, gloves, camera battery topped up, lens cloth, ¥10,000 cash.

## Rain plan B
Swap Hida-no-Sato for **Kusakabe Folk Museum** (1879 merchant house, ¥500, 9:00–16:30, closed Tue) + **Yoshijima Heritage House** (same street, ¥500) for two hours of indoor traditional architecture, then **Takayama Museum of History and Art** (free, 9:00–19:00). Skip Higashiyama if it's pouring; it's slippery and the temples blur in fog.

## Photography golden hour
- **Sunrise (~6:25):** Nakabashi Bridge over the Miyagawa, looking upstream toward the Hida Alps. Frost on the railings if cold enough.
- **Sunset (~17:10):** From the Shiroyama Park overlook on the Higashiyama loop — Takayama rooftops with the alps backlit.
- **Specific shot:** Hida-no-Sato — the upper pond with the Wakayama gassho-zukuri reflected, maples behind. Time it for ~14:30–15:30 when the western light hits the thatch directly.

## Reservations needed for this day
- None mandatory. Suzuya and Ebisu don't take reservations. Hyotei takes phone reservations day-of.

## Off-path notes
- **Skip the sake breweries' tasting rooms** as a matter of stated trip rules, but if you want one *kura* interior, **Funasaka Shuzō** on Kamisannomachi has the most theatrical lobby (timber beams, lacquered counter, free roasted rice tea served to non-drinkers in a fired-clay cup — ask politely).
- **Hirata Kinenkan** (Hirata Folk Museum, ¥300, 9:00–16:30, closed Wed) — tiny merchant-family museum almost no foreign tourist enters. Lacquerware, sarubobo prototypes, household ledgers. 20 min, in passing.
- **Coffee #2:** **Cafe Aoteo** (in a 130-year-old machiya, 35-2 Kamiichinomachi) for the afternoon dip. Hand-drip, jazz on vinyl, no laptops. Closed Thu.
- The *sarubobo* doll Sonja will see everywhere has no face on purpose — historically because it expressed the wearer's emotion. The red ones at the markets are usually machine-made; **Sumikura Hirohito** (small studio on Yasugawa-dōri) hand-stitches them. ¥1,500 small, ¥4,000 large.

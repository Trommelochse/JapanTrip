# Practical — Hakone

## Hakone Free Pass

**¥7,100 for 2 days from Shinjuku** (includes Odakyu round trip Shinjuku ↔ Hakone-Yumoto in base class, plus unlimited use of all Hakone Tozan trains, cable car, ropeway, sightseeing cruise on Lake Ashi, and Hakone Tozan buses for 2 days). 2026 price verified May 17 via Odakyu.

- The Romancecar limited-express seat is **extra** (¥1,200 each way). Buy the seat reservation separately when you buy the pass.
- Buy via the **EMot app** (English, Apple/Google Pay) before leaving Tokyo. Show the QR at the Hakone-Yumoto gate. Paper version still sold at Odakyu Sightseeing Service Center in Shinjuku Station west exit if you prefer.
- For this 2-day, 2-night itinerary, the pass pays itself off easily: Shinjuku round trip alone is ¥2,560 base + the Hakone leg-by-leg fares for the cable car (¥430), ropeway (¥1,500), cruise (¥1,200), and Tozan train (¥460) add up well past ¥7,100.

## Romancecar booking

- **Reservations open 1 month before the date at 10:00 JST sharp.** For Oct 28 outbound, the seat window opens Sept 28 at 10:00 JST.
- Book at [e-Romancecar](https://www.web-odakyu.com/e-romancecar/?language=en) (English, takes foreign cards) or in the EMot app.
- **Train to target:** "Super Hakone 11" or similar ~11:00 departure from Shinjuku, arriving Hakone-Yumoto ~12:20.
- **Seat choice:** Right-hand window (seats A) for Tanzawa mountains. The GSE 70000 series has front-row observation seats facing the windshield — if any are available in row 1 car 1 or car 7, grab them. The kids love them, so they go fast on weekends; weekday October is fine.
- **Digital "Ticketless" ticket is ¥50 cheaper** than paper at the station.

## Luggage forwarding (Takkyubin)

- **The move:** Send your main suitcases **Tokyo hotel → Takayama hotel directly**, bypassing Hakone. Travel with a small overnight bag for the ryokan.
- Drop bags at the Tokyo hotel front desk by **09:30 on Day 6 (Oct 28)** for next-day delivery to Takayama Day 8 (Oct 30) afternoon. Most hotels handle the form and the call to Yamato; you pay at the front desk and walk out with just your daypack.
- **Cost:** ¥2,000–2,500 per suitcase (size L, under 25 kg). The Tokyo → Takayama route takes 1 full transit day, so Day 6 morning drop = Day 7 evening arrival = waiting at your Takayama hotel when you get there Day 8.
- The ryokan will also handle forwarding if you change your mind (luggage goes from ryokan → Takayama for same pricing) — just ask at check-in.

## Weather and the typhoon plan

- Late October is the **tail of typhoon season** for Hakone. Direct hits are rare by Oct 28, but heavy-rain bands from passing systems are common.
- **The ropeway closes at sustained wind >30 m/s** or for volcanic activity at Owakudani. Check [hakoneropeway.co.jp/en](https://www.hakoneropeway.co.jp/en/) at breakfast on Day 7.
- **Rain plan B is in day-07-hakone.md** — Pola Museum + Lalique + Open-Air covered sections handle a fully washed-out day.

## Cash and cards

- Splurge ryokans accept all major cards including AmEx. The Hakone Free Pass via EMot app takes any card. Local restaurants in Gora and Moto-Hakone are often cash-only — carry ¥15,000–20,000 in cash per person for the two days.
- The ryokan settles all incidentals (extra in-room meals, gift shop, kashikiri-buro private bath bookings) on check-out, on card.

## Phone, language, etiquette

- Splurge ryokans all have English-capable front desks. The in-room attendant may have limited English — speak slowly, use Google Translate for menu questions. Asking about the kaiseki courses is welcome; the attendant will often go fetch the chef if your interest seems genuine.
- **Onsen etiquette:** wash and rinse fully at the seated showers before entering the bath. No swimsuits. No towel in the water. Tie long hair up. No phones in any onsen area, ever.
- **No tipping. Anywhere. Including the ryokan.** A short thank-you and a small bow at check-out is the correct gesture.
